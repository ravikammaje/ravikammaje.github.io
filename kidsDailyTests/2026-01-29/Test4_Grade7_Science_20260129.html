Section C: Long Answer Questions (5 x 4 = 20 Marks)

Answer the following questions in detail.

1. Sound and its properties: Physics [4]

a) What is sound? How is it produced?

b) Explain why sound cannot travel through a vacuum.

c) What is an echo? Under what conditions can an echo be heard?

d) A thunder is heard 5 seconds after lightning is seen. If the speed of sound in air is 340 m/s, how far is the thunderstorm?

2. Atomic Structure and Valency: Chemistry [4]

a) What is an atom? Name the three subatomic particles and their charges.

b) Define valency. What determines the valency of an element?

c) Write the symbols and valencies of: Calcium, Chlorine, Nitrogen, and Sulphur.

d) Write the chemical formulae of: (i) Calcium chloride (ii) Aluminium oxide

3. Plant and Animal Tissues: Biology [4]

a) What is a tissue? Why are tissues important in living organisms?

b) Name the four types of animal tissues. Write one function of each.

c) Describe the structure and function of xylem tissue in plants.

d) What is the difference between voluntary and involuntary muscles? Give one example of each.

4. Heat Transfer: Physics [4]

a) Define the three modes of heat transfer: conduction, convection, and radiation.

b) Why are cooking utensils made of metal but have wooden or plastic handles?

c) Explain how a sea breeze and land breeze are formed using the concept of convection.

d) Give two examples of heat transfer by radiation.

5. Weathering and Rock Formation: Geography [4]

a) What is weathering? Name the three types of weathering.

b) Describe how igneous rocks are formed. Give two examples of igneous rocks.

c) How are sedimentary rocks different from metamorphic rocks? Give one example of each.

d) Why is soil conservation important? Name two methods of soil conservation.
