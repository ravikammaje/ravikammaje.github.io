Section A: Multiple Choice Questions (5 x 2 = 10 Marks)

1. What is 56 divided by 8?

Answer: b) 7

56 / 8 = 7

2. What fraction of this shape is shaded?

Answer: b) 2/4

2 parts out of 4 = 2/4 (or 1/2)

3. How many centimeters are there in 2 meters?

Answer: b) 200 cm

1 m = 100 cm, so 2 m = 200 cm

4. If 9 x 6 = 54, then 54 divided by 9 = ?

Answer: b) 6

Division is the inverse of multiplication

5. Change from Rs 20?

Answer: b) Rs 7

8 + 5 = 13, Change = 20 - 13 = Rs 7

Section B: Short Answer Questions (5 x 2 = 10 Marks)

1. Divide 72 by 9 and check your answer using multiplication. [2]

Answer:

72 / 9 = 8

Check: 8 x 9 = 72 (Correct)

2. Write the fraction for: "Five out of eight equal parts." [2]

Answer:

Fraction = 5/8 (five-eighths)

Drawing should show a shape divided into 8 equal parts with 5 parts shaded.

3. A ribbon is 3 m 45 cm long. Express this length in centimeters only. [2]

Answer:

3 m = 3 x 100 = 300 cm

Total = 300 + 45 = 345 cm

4. Rahul has Rs 50. He wants to buy 7 candies that cost Rs 6 each. Does he have enough money? [2]

Answer:

Cost of 7 candies = 7 x 6 = Rs 42

Rahul has Rs 50, and 50 > 42

Yes, he has enough money. (He will have Rs 8 left over)

5. When 35 is divided by 4, what is the quotient and remainder? [2]

Answer:

35 / 4 = 8 remainder 3

Quotient = 8, Remainder = 3

Check: 8 x 4 = 32, and 32 + 3 = 35

Section C: Long Answer Questions (5 x 4 = 20 Marks)

1. A farmer has 96 mangoes to pack into 8 baskets. [4]

a) Mangoes in each basket:

96 / 8 = 12 mangoes per basket

b) Good mangoes in each basket after removing rotten ones:

12 - 15 = This is not possible (cannot have negative mangoes)

Note: If the question means 15 total rotten across all baskets, then each basket has 12 mangoes.

Assuming 1-2 rotten per basket: 12 - 2 = 10 good mangoes per basket (example)

If literally 15 from each basket: The question has an error as each basket only has 12.

Corrected interpretation: If 15 mangoes total are rotten:

Good mangoes total = 96 - 15 = 81 mangoes

c) Total good mangoes:

If 15 total rotten: 96 - 15 = 81 good mangoes

2. Fractions: 1/4, 2/4, 3/4 [4]

a) Drawings: Three rectangles, each divided into 4 equal parts.

- First rectangle: 1 part shaded (1/4)

- Second rectangle: 2 parts shaded (2/4)

- Third rectangle: 3 parts shaded (3/4)

b) Largest fraction: 3/4, Smallest fraction: 1/4

c) To make 1/4 equal to 3/4:

3/4 - 1/4 = 2/4 (or 1/2)

Need to add 2/4 to 1/4

d) Yes, 2/4 = 1/2

If you divide a shape into 4 parts and shade 2, it covers the same area as dividing into 2 parts and shading 1.

3. Meera's house: 2 km 350 m, Raj's house: 1 km 800 m [4]

a) Convert to meters:

Meera: 2 km 350 m = (2 x 1000) + 350 = 2000 + 350 = 2350 m

Raj: 1 km 800 m = (1 x 1000) + 800 = 1000 + 800 = 1800 m

b) Whose house is closer?

Raj's house is closer (1800 m < 2350 m)

Difference = 2350 - 1800 = 550 m closer

c) Meera's total walking distance:

To school and back = 2350 x 2 = 4700 m

d) Time for Meera to reach school:

500 m in 10 minutes

2350 m will take: (2350 / 500) x 10 = 4.7 x 10 = 47 minutes

4. Stationery shop problem [4]

a) Amit's spending:

2 notebooks = 2 x 35 = Rs 70

3 pens = 3 x 12 = Rs 36

Total = 70 + 36 = Rs 106

b) Change from Rs 150:

Change = 150 - 106 = Rs 44

c) Sneha's purchase:

1 notebook = Rs 35

2 rulers = 2 x 8 = Rs 16

Total spent = 35 + 16 = Rs 51

Remaining = 100 - 51 = Rs 49

Each sharpener = Rs 5

Number of sharpeners = 49 / 5 = 9 remainder 4

She can buy 9 sharpeners (with Rs 4 left over)

5. Teacher distributing 85 chocolates among 7 students [4]

a) Chocolates per student:

85 / 7 = 12 remainder 1

Each student gets 12 chocolates

b) Chocolates left over:

Remainder = 1 chocolate

c) With 6 more chocolates:

Total = 85 + 6 = 91 chocolates

91 / 7 = 13 remainder 0

Yes, 91 divides equally - each student gets 13 chocolates with no remainder.

d) Smallest number of extra chocolates needed:

Current remainder = 1

To make it divisible by 7: need 7 - 1 = 6 more chocolates

Minimum extra chocolates = 6
