Section C: Long Answer Questions (5 x 4 = 20 Marks)

Show all your working clearly.

1. A farmer has 96 mangoes. He wants to pack them equally into 8 baskets.

a) How many mangoes will be in each basket?

b) If 15 mangoes from each basket are rotten, how many good mangoes are in each basket?

c) How many good mangoes are there in total across all 8 baskets? [4]

2. Look at these fractions: 1/4, 2/4, 3/4

a) Draw three identical rectangles and shade each one to show 1/4, 2/4, and 3/4.

b) Which fraction is the largest? Which is the smallest?

c) What fraction would you need to add to 1/4 to make it equal to 3/4?

d) Is 2/4 the same as 1/2? Explain using a drawing. [4]

3. Meera's house is 2 km 350 m from school. Raj's house is 1 km 800 m from school.

a) Convert both distances to meters only.

b) Whose house is closer to school? By how many meters?

c) If Meera walks to school and back home, what is the total distance she walks in meters?

d) If Meera walks 500 m in 10 minutes, how long will it take her to reach school? [4]

4. At a stationery shop:

- Notebook costs Rs 35

- Pen costs Rs 12

- Ruler costs Rs 8

- Sharpener costs Rs 5

a) Amit buys 2 notebooks and 3 pens. How much does he spend?

b) If Amit gives Rs 150, how much change does he get?

c) Sneha has Rs 100. She buys 1 notebook, 2 rulers, and some sharpeners. How many sharpeners can she buy with the remaining money? [4]

5. A teacher has 85 chocolates to distribute equally among 7 students.

a) How many chocolates will each student get?

b) How many chocolates will be left over?

c) If the teacher buys 6 more chocolates, can she now divide them equally among the 7 students with no remainder? Show your working.

d) What is the smallest number of extra chocolates she needs to buy so that there is no remainder? [4]
