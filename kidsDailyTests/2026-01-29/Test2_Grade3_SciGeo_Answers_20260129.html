Grade 3 ICSE Science and Geography Test - ANSWER KEY

Date: 29 January 2026

Total Marks: 40
Time: 25-30 minutes
Section A: Multiple Choice Questions (5 x 2 = 10 Marks)

1. Which part of a plant makes food using sunlight? Science

Answer: c) Leaf

Leaves have chlorophyll and perform photosynthesis.

2. The Taj Mahal is located in which city? Geography

Answer: b) Agra

Taj Mahal is in Agra, Uttar Pradesh.

3. What is the main source of energy for the Earth? Science

Answer: c) Sun

The Sun provides light and heat energy to Earth.

4. How many seasons are there in India? Geography

Answer: c) 4

Summer, Monsoon (Rainy), Autumn, Winter (some consider 6 with Spring and Late Autumn)

5. Which type of root does a carrot plant have? Science

Answer: b) Tap root

Carrot has a single main root that grows deep.

Section B: Short Answer Questions (5 x 2 = 10 Marks)

1. Name the four main parts of a plant and write one function of each. Science [2]

Answer:

1. Root: Absorbs water and minerals from soil, anchors the plant

2. Stem: Supports the plant, transports water and food

3. Leaf: Makes food through photosynthesis

4. Flower: Helps in reproduction, produces seeds

2. Name any three famous monuments of India and the cities where they are located. Geography [2]

Answer:

1. Taj Mahal - Agra

2. Red Fort - Delhi

3. Gateway of India - Mumbai

(Other acceptable answers: Qutub Minar - Delhi, India Gate - Delhi, Hawa Mahal - Jaipur, etc.)

3. Why is the Sun important for plants? Give two reasons. Science [2]

Answer:

1. Plants need sunlight to make food through photosynthesis.

2. Sunlight helps plants grow strong and healthy.

(Also acceptable: Sun provides warmth for plant growth, helps in flowering)

4. Name the four main seasons and write one month when each occurs in India. Geography [2]

Answer:

1. Summer: May

2. Monsoon (Rainy): July

3. Autumn: October

4. Winter: December

5. What is the difference between a tap root and a fibrous root? Give one example of each. Science [2]

Answer:

Tap Root: Has one main root that grows deep into the soil with smaller roots branching from it.

Example: Carrot, Radish, Mango tree

Fibrous Root: Has many thin roots of similar size spreading out from the base of the stem.

Example: Grass, Wheat, Rice

Section C: Long Answer Questions (5 x 4 = 20 Marks)

1. Plants have different parts that help them survive. Science [4]

a) Drawing: Student should draw a plant showing roots (underground), stem, leaves, and flower. Each part should be clearly labeled.

b) Function of stem:

- Supports the plant and holds it upright

- Transports water and minerals from roots to leaves

- Carries food made in leaves to other parts of the plant

c) How roots help the plant:

- Absorb water and minerals from the soil

- Anchor the plant firmly in the ground

- Store food in some plants (like carrot, radish)

d) Why leaves have stomata:

- Stomata are tiny pores/holes on leaves

- They allow exchange of gases (taking in carbon dioxide and releasing oxygen)

- They help in transpiration (release of water vapor)

2. India has many beautiful and historical monuments. Geography [4]

a) Who built the Taj Mahal and why:

- Emperor Shah Jahan built the Taj Mahal

- It was built in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal

- It is a symbol of love and is made of white marble

b) Monument in Delhi for Independence Day flag hoisting:

- Red Fort (Lal Qila)

- The Prime Minister hoists the national flag here every year on 15th August

c) Gateway of India:

- Located in Mumbai, Maharashtra

- Built to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary in 1911

- Famous as a symbol of Mumbai and overlooks the Arabian Sea

d) Ancient temple in India:

- Konark Sun Temple - Odisha

- Brihadeeswarar Temple - Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu

- Meenakshi Temple - Madurai, Tamil Nadu

(Any ancient temple with correct location is acceptable)

3. The Sun is called the natural source of light and energy. Science [4]

a) Solar energy and its uses:

- Solar energy is the energy we get from the Sun in the form of light and heat

Uses:

1. Solar panels convert sunlight into electricity

2. Solar water heaters warm water using sunlight

(Also: drying clothes, cooking with solar cookers)

b) Why we have day and night:

- Earth rotates (spins) on its axis

- One complete rotation takes 24 hours

- The side facing the Sun has day (receives sunlight)

- The side away from the Sun has night (no sunlight)

c) What would happen without the Sun:

- Earth would become very cold and dark

- Plants could not make food (no photosynthesis)

- All living things would eventually die

- Water would freeze

d) Things that need sunlight to survive:

1. Plants (for photosynthesis and growth)

2. Animals (for warmth and to find food)

(Also: Humans need sunlight for Vitamin D)

4. Earth experiences different seasons during the year. Geography [4]

a) Why we have different seasons:

- Earth revolves (moves in an orbit) around the Sun

- Earth's axis is tilted at an angle

- As Earth moves, different parts receive more or less direct sunlight

- This causes seasons to change throughout the year

b) Weather during summer season in India:

- Very hot temperatures (can reach 40-45Â°C in many places)

- Days are longer, nights are shorter

- Dry and sunny weather

- People wear light cotton clothes

c) Monsoon season:

- Heavy rainfall occurs during monsoon (June to September)

- Winds bring rain from the Indian Ocean

- Important for farmers because:

- Rain fills rivers and provides water for crops

- Rice, sugarcane, and many crops depend on monsoon rain

- Groundwater gets recharged

d) Season for woolen clothes:

- Winter season (December to February)

- We wear woolen clothes because the weather is cold

- Wool keeps our body warm by trapping heat

5. Plants provide us with many useful things. Science [4]

a) Fruits and vegetables from plants:

Fruits: Mango, Apple, Banana (also: Orange, Grapes, Papaya)

Vegetables: Potato, Tomato, Spinach (also: Carrot, Cauliflower, Beans)

b) What we get from sugarcane stem:

- Sugar (gur/jaggery)

- Sugarcane juice

c) Two spices and the plant parts they come from:

1. Clove - comes from flower buds

2. Ginger - comes from underground stem (rhizome)

(Also: Cinnamon - bark, Pepper - fruit, Turmeric - rhizome, Cumin - seeds)

d) How plants help the environment:

1. Plants release oxygen which we breathe

2. Plants absorb carbon dioxide and help clean the air

(Also: Plants provide shade, prevent soil erosion, give shelter to animals, maintain water cycle)
