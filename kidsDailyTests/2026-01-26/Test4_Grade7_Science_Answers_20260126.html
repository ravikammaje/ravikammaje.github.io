Section A: Multiple Choice Questions (5 × 2 = 10 Marks)

1. Which type of tissue is responsible for transporting water in plants? Biology

Answer: b) Xylem

Xylem transports water and minerals from roots to leaves. Phloem transports food from leaves to other parts.

2. What is the SI unit of speed? Physics

Answer: b) m/s

The SI unit of speed is metres per second (m/s). km/h is a commonly used unit but not the SI unit.

3. Which of the following is NOT a physical change? Chemistry

Answer: c) Burning of paper

Burning is a chemical change as new substances (ash, CO₂, water vapor) are formed. The other options are reversible physical changes.

4. The ozone layer is found in which layer of the atmosphere? Geography

Answer: b) Stratosphere

The ozone layer is located in the stratosphere, about 15-35 km above Earth's surface. It protects us from harmful UV radiation.

5. Which part of the human brain controls balance and coordination? Biology

Answer: b) Cerebellum

The cerebellum is located at the back of the brain and controls balance, posture, and coordination of movements.

Section B: Short Answer Questions (5 × 2 = 10 Marks)

1. State two differences between physical changes and chemical changes. Chemistry [2]

Answer:

Physical Change Chemical Change
No new substance is formed New substance(s) are formed
Usually reversible Usually irreversible

Other valid differences: Change in color/odor/heat in chemical changes; only physical properties change in physical changes.

2. What is photosynthesis? Write the word equation for photosynthesis. Biology [2]

Answer:

Definition: Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants make their own food (glucose) using sunlight, carbon dioxide, and water in the presence of chlorophyll.

Word Equation:

Carbon dioxide + Water → Glucose + Oxygen

(in presence of sunlight and chlorophyll)

3. Define speed and write its formula. If a car travels 150 km in 3 hours, calculate its speed. Physics [2]

Answer:

Definition: Speed is the distance travelled by an object per unit time.

Formula: Speed = Distance / Time

Calculation:

Speed = 150 km / 3 hours = 50 km/h

Speed of the car = 50 km/h

4. Name the three types of rocks with one example of each. Geography [2]

Answer:

1. Igneous Rocks: Formed by cooling and solidification of molten magma/lava.

Example: Granite, Basalt

2. Sedimentary Rocks: Formed by deposition and compaction of sediments over time.

Example: Sandstone, Limestone, Shale

3. Metamorphic Rocks: Formed when existing rocks are changed by heat and pressure.

Example: Marble (from limestone), Slate (from shale)

5. What is the valency of the following elements: Sodium (Na), Oxygen (O), Aluminium (Al)? Chemistry [2]

Answer:

Sodium (Na): Valency = 1 (loses 1 electron to form Na⁺)

Oxygen (O): Valency = 2 (gains 2 electrons to form O²⁻)

Aluminium (Al): Valency = 3 (loses 3 electrons to form Al³⁺)

Valency is the combining capacity of an element, determined by the number of electrons it can lose, gain, or share.

Section C: Problem Solving (5 × 4 = 20 Marks)

1. Explain the process of reflection of light. State the two laws of reflection. Draw a diagram. Physics [4]

Reflection of Light:

Reflection is the bouncing back of light when it falls on a smooth, polished surface like a mirror. The light ray that falls on the surface is called the incident ray, and the ray that bounces back is called the reflected ray.

Laws of Reflection:

First Law: The incident ray, the reflected ray, and the normal at the point of incidence all lie in the same plane.

Second Law: The angle of incidence is equal to the angle of reflection (∠i = ∠r).

Diagram should show:

- A plane mirror (horizontal line)

- Normal (perpendicular to mirror at point of incidence)

- Incident ray (coming towards mirror)

- Reflected ray (going away from mirror)

- Angle of incidence (i) and Angle of reflection (r) marked and equal

(Award marks for explanation + 2 laws + correct diagram with labels)

2. Describe the structure and functions of the human excretory system. Biology [4]

Main Organs of the Excretory System:

1. Two Kidneys: Bean-shaped organs located on either side of the spine.

2. Two Ureters: Tubes that carry urine from kidneys to bladder.

3. Urinary Bladder: Muscular sac that stores urine.

4. Urethra: Tube through which urine is expelled from the body.

Functions of Kidneys:

1. Filtration: Blood is filtered to remove waste products like urea, uric acid, and excess salts.

2. Reabsorption: Useful substances like glucose, amino acids, and water are reabsorbed back into the blood.

3. Urine Formation: The remaining waste and excess water form urine.

4. Maintaining Water Balance: Kidneys regulate the amount of water in the body.

5. Maintaining Salt Balance: Control the concentration of salts in body fluids.

(Award marks for naming organs + explaining kidney functions)

4. Aerobic and anaerobic respiration. Biology [4]

Aerobic Respiration:

- Occurs in the presence of oxygen

- Takes place in the mitochondria of cells

- Releases more energy (38 ATP molecules)

- Complete breakdown of glucose

Word Equation:

Glucose + Oxygen → Carbon dioxide + Water + Energy

Anaerobic Respiration:

- Occurs in the absence of oxygen

- Takes place in the cytoplasm

- Releases less energy (2 ATP molecules)

- Incomplete breakdown of glucose

Word Equation (in animals/muscles):

Glucose → Lactic acid + Energy

Word Equation (in yeast/plants):

Glucose → Ethanol + Carbon dioxide + Energy

Examples: Anaerobic respiration occurs in muscle cells during vigorous exercise (causing cramps due to lactic acid) and in yeast during fermentation.

5. Electric circuit, conductors, insulators, and tungsten filament. Physics [4]

a) Circuit Diagram should include:

- Cell/Battery (with + and - terminals marked)

- Switch (open or closed)

- Bulb

- Connecting wires forming a complete loop

- Proper symbols for each component

b) Conductor vs Insulator:

Conductor: A material that allows electric current to pass through it easily.

Examples: Copper, Aluminium, Iron, Silver, Gold

Insulator: A material that does not allow electric current to pass through it.

Examples: Rubber, Plastic, Wood, Glass, Paper

c) Why is tungsten used for bulb filaments?

1. Tungsten has a very high melting point (3422°C), so it does not melt when heated.

2. It can withstand high temperatures and glow brightly without burning.

3. It has high resistivity, which helps in producing heat and light.
