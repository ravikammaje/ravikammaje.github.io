Grade 3 ICSE Science and Geography Test - ANSWER KEY

Date: 26 January 2026

Total Marks: 40
Time: 25-30 minutes
Section A: Multiple Choice Questions (5 × 2 = 10 Marks)

1. Which of these is a characteristic of living things? Science

Answer: b) They need food and water

Living things need food, water, and air to survive. They grow, breathe, move, and reproduce.

2. How many continents are there on Earth? Geography

Answer: c) 7

The 7 continents are: Asia, Africa, North America, South America, Antarctica, Europe, and Australia.

3. Which part of a plant makes food? Science

Answer: c) Leaves

Leaves contain chlorophyll and make food for the plant through photosynthesis using sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide.

4. What is the national bird of India? Geography

Answer: b) Peacock

The Indian Peacock (Pavo cristatus) was declared the National Bird of India in 1963.

5. Water in which state can be poured? Science

Answer: b) Liquid

Liquids can flow and be poured. Solids have a fixed shape and gases spread out in all directions.

Section B: Short Answer Questions (5 × 2 = 10 Marks)

1. Name the four cardinal directions. Geography [2]

Answer: North, South, East, West

These are the four main directions used for navigation. The Sun rises in the East and sets in the West.

2. Write any two differences between living things and non-living things. Science [2]

Answer:

1. Living things grow and develop, but non-living things do not grow.

2. Living things need food to survive, but non-living things do not need food.

(Other valid differences: breathing, reproduction, movement, response to stimuli)

3. Name the largest continent and the largest ocean. Geography [2]

Answer: Largest continent: Asia; Largest ocean: Pacific Ocean

Asia covers about 30% of Earth's land area. The Pacific Ocean is the largest and deepest ocean, covering about one-third of Earth's surface.

4. What are the three states of matter? Give one example of each. Science [2]

Answer:

1. Solid - Example: Ice, stone, wood, book

2. Liquid - Example: Water, milk, juice, oil

3. Gas - Example: Air, oxygen, steam, carbon dioxide

5. Why does the Sun appear to rise in the East and set in the West? Geography [2]

Answer: The Earth rotates on its axis from West to East. Because of this rotation, the Sun appears to rise in the East and set in the West.

The Sun does not actually move around the Earth - it is Earth's rotation that creates this appearance.

Section C: Problem Solving (5 × 4 = 20 Marks)

1. Draw and label the parts of a plant. Explain the function of any two parts. Science [4]

Parts of a plant: Roots, Stem, Leaves, Flowers, Fruits

Functions (any two):

1. Roots: Roots hold the plant firmly in the soil. They absorb water and minerals from the soil for the plant.

2. Stem: The stem supports the plant and carries water and nutrients from roots to leaves. It also carries food from leaves to other parts.

3. Leaves: Leaves make food for the plant through photosynthesis using sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide.

4. Flowers: Flowers help in reproduction. They produce seeds and fruits.

(Award marks for correct diagram + labeling + 2 functions explained)

2. What causes day and night? Explain with the help of a simple diagram showing Earth and the Sun. Geography [4]

Cause of Day and Night:

1. The Earth rotates (spins) on its imaginary axis from West to East.

2. The Earth takes 24 hours to complete one rotation.

3. The part of Earth facing the Sun experiences day (sunlight).

4. The part of Earth facing away from the Sun experiences night (darkness).

5. As Earth keeps rotating, different parts experience day and night.

Diagram should show: The Sun on one side, Earth in the middle, with one half lit (day) and one half dark (night), and an arrow showing rotation direction.

(Award marks for explanation + correct diagram)

3. Describe the water cycle. Include the following words in your answer: evaporation, condensation, precipitation. Science [4]

The Water Cycle:

1. Evaporation: The Sun heats water in oceans, rivers, lakes, and ponds. The water turns into water vapor (gas) and rises up into the air.

2. Condensation: When water vapor rises high in the sky, it becomes cool. The cool water vapor changes back into tiny water droplets. These droplets come together to form clouds.

3. Precipitation: When the clouds become heavy with water droplets, water falls back to Earth as rain, snow, or hail. This is called precipitation.

4. The water collects in rivers, lakes, and oceans, and the cycle starts again.

(Award marks for correct use of all three terms and clear explanation)

4. Name any four national symbols of India. Write one line about each symbol. Geography [4]

National Symbols of India (any four):

1. National Flag (Tiranga): The Indian flag has three colors - saffron, white, and green, with the Ashoka Chakra in the center.

2. National Emblem: The Lion Capital of Ashoka with four lions, adopted from Sarnath, with the motto "Satyameva Jayate" (Truth Alone Triumphs).

3. National Bird (Peacock): The beautiful Indian Peacock with colorful feathers represents grace and beauty.

4. National Animal (Bengal Tiger): The Royal Bengal Tiger represents strength and power of India.

5. National Flower (Lotus): The lotus symbolizes purity and beauty as it blooms in muddy water but remains clean.

6. National Anthem: "Jana Gana Mana" written by Rabindranath Tagore.

(Award 1 mark for each correctly named symbol with description, max 4)

5. Why is the Sun important for life on Earth? Write any four reasons. How can we use solar energy in our daily life? Science [4]

Importance of the Sun (any four):

1. The Sun gives us light during the day so we can see.

2. The Sun provides heat to keep Earth warm enough for living things.

3. Plants need sunlight to make food through photosynthesis.

4. The Sun helps in the water cycle by evaporating water.

5. Sunlight helps our body make Vitamin D for strong bones.

6. The Sun causes day and night and different seasons.

Uses of Solar Energy in Daily Life:

1. Solar panels on rooftops to generate electricity

2. Solar water heaters to heat water

3. Solar cookers to cook food

4. Solar calculators and watches

5. Solar street lights

(Award marks for 4 reasons + uses of solar energy)
