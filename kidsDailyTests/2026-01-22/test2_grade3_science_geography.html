Section C: Long Answer Questions (20 marks)

11. The water cycle (4 marks - award 1 mark for explaining the concept, 1 mark for each of 3 steps):
The water cycle is the continuous movement of water on Earth.
Steps:
1. Evaporation: Water from oceans, rivers, and lakes turns into water vapor due to heat from the sun
2. Condensation: Water vapor rises up, cools down, and forms clouds
3. Precipitation: Water falls back to earth as rain, snow, or hail
(Accept any additional valid step like collection/runoff)

12. Main parts of a plant (4 marks - 1 mark for each part with function):
1. Roots: Absorb water and minerals from the soil and hold the plant firmly in the ground
2. Stem: Carries water and nutrients to all parts of the plant and supports the plant
3. Leaves: Make food for the plant through photosynthesis
4. Flowers: Help in reproduction and produce seeds/fruits

13. Three ways to save water at home (4 marks):
1. Turn off the tap while brushing teeth or washing hands with soap
2. Fix leaking taps and pipes immediately to prevent wastage
3. Use a bucket instead of a shower for bathing, or take shorter showers
(Accept other valid methods like: reusing water used for washing vegetables to water plants, using washing machine only with full load, etc.)

14. What is a map and its uses (4 marks):
A map is a drawing or picture that shows features of an area as seen from above, like roads, rivers, mountains, and cities.
Uses of maps:
1. To find directions and locate places
2. To measure distances between places
(Accept other valid uses: planning journeys, understanding geography of a place, finding boundaries between countries/states, etc.)

15. Why living things need food (4 marks):
All living things need food to survive and grow. Food provides energy that living things use to carry out all life processes like breathing, moving, and growing.
What plants do with food: Plants make their own food through photosynthesis and use it for growth and energy.
What animals do with food: Animals eat plants or other animals to get food, which they digest to get energy and nutrients for their body to work properly.
(Award marks for explaining need for food and what both plants and animals do with it)
