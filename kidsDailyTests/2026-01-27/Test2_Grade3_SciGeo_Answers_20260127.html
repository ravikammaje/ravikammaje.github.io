Section B: Short Answer Questions [2 marks each]

Write your answers in the space provided.

1. Name any two rivers of India and tell which states they flow through. Geography [2]

Answer: Ganga flows through Uttarakhand, Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, and West Bengal. Yamuna flows through Uttarakhand, Haryana, Delhi, and Uttar Pradesh. (Any two rivers with correct states accepted.)

2. Name the five sense organs and the sense each one is used for. Science [2]

Answer: Eyes (sight), Ears (hearing), Nose (smell), Tongue (taste), Skin (touch).

3. What is the difference between natural environment and human-made environment? Give one example of each. Geography [2]

Answer: Natural environment includes things made by nature (e.g., rivers, mountains, forests). Human-made environment includes things made by humans (e.g., buildings, roads, bridges).

4. Name any two spices and two pulses that we get from plants. Science [2]

Answer: Spices: turmeric, pepper/chilli/cardamom (any two). Pulses: lentils (dal), chickpeas/rajma/moong (any two).

5. Write three things you can do to help reduce pollution. Geography [2]

Answer: Any three from: Plant more trees, use less plastic, walk or cycle instead of driving, don't burn garbage, use dustbins, save water, reduce/reuse/recycle.
Section C: Long Answer Questions [4 marks each]

Answer the following questions in detail.

1. Our body has a skeletal system made up of bones. Science [4]

a) What does the skeletal system do for our body? (Write two functions)

b) Name the bone that protects our brain.

c) How many bones does an adult human body have?

d) Why should we drink milk for strong bones?

Answer:
a) The skeletal system gives shape and support to our body, and it protects our internal organs.
b) The skull protects our brain.
c) An adult human body has 206 bones.
d) Milk contains calcium which makes our bones strong and healthy.

2. India has many different types of physical features. Geography [4]

a) Name the mountain range in the north of India.

b) Name any two deserts found in India.

c) India is surrounded by water on three sides. Name the water bodies on the east, west, and south.

d) Why are rivers important for India?

Answer:
a) The Himalayas.
b) Thar Desert (Rajasthan) and the cold desert of Ladakh (any two deserts).
c) East: Bay of Bengal, West: Arabian Sea, South: Indian Ocean.
d) Rivers provide water for drinking, farming, and irrigation; they support transportation and are home to many animals and plants.

3. Birds have different types of beaks and feet depending on what they eat and where they live. Science [4]

a) What type of beak does an eagle have and why?

b) What type of feet does a duck have and why?

c) Name a bird that uses its beak to make holes in tree trunks. What does it find there?

d) Why do birds migrate? Name one bird that migrates to India.

Answer:
a) An eagle has a sharp, hooked beak to tear meat from its prey.
b) A duck has webbed feet that help it swim in water.
c) A woodpecker uses its strong, pointed beak to make holes in tree trunks to find insects.
d) Birds migrate to find warmer weather and food during winter. Siberian crane is a bird that migrates to India (also acceptable: flamingo, painted stork).

4. Pollution is harmful to our environment. Geography [4]

a) What are the three main types of pollution?

b) Give two causes of water pollution.

c) How does air pollution affect our health?

d) Explain what "Reduce, Reuse, Recycle" means with one example for each.

Answer:
a) Air pollution, water pollution, and land pollution.
b) Throwing garbage and chemicals into rivers/lakes, factory waste going into water bodies.
c) Air pollution causes breathing problems, coughing, asthma, and other lung diseases.
d) Reduce: Use less plastic bags. Reuse: Use old jars to store things. Recycle: Turn old newspapers into new paper products.

5. Good hygiene and cleanliness keep us healthy. Science [4]

a) Why should we wash our hands before eating?

b) Name two diseases that can spread due to dirty surroundings.

c) Write three healthy habits every student should follow.

d) Why is it important to brush our teeth twice a day?

Answer:
a) We should wash our hands to remove germs and dirt that can cause diseases if they enter our body through food.
b) Malaria (from mosquitoes in dirty water), cholera/typhoid/diarrhea (any two).
c) Any three: Bathe daily, cut nails regularly, eat fresh food, drink clean water, exercise regularly, sleep on time, wash hands frequently.
d) Brushing removes food particles and bacteria from teeth, prevents cavities and tooth decay, and keeps gums healthy.
