1. Which of these rational numbers is the smallest? −3/4, −1/2, −5/8, −7/8 [2]

a) −3/4
b) −1/2
c) −5/8
d) −7/8 ✔
Answer: d) −7/8
Converting to common denominator 8: −6/8, −4/8, −5/8, −7/8. The smallest is −7/8.

2. If A = {2, 4, 6, 8, 10} and B = {1, 3, 5, 7, 9}, what is n(A) + n(B)? [2]

a) 5
b) 8
c) 10 ✔
d) 25
Answer: c) 10
n(A) = 5 (five elements), n(B) = 5 (five elements). n(A) + n(B) = 5 + 5 = 10.

3. If 3 : 5 = x : 35, what is the value of x? [2]

a) 15
b) 21 ✔
c) 25
d) 105
Answer: b) 21
3/5 = x/35. Cross multiply: x = (3 × 35) / 5 = 105/5 = 21.

4. The area of a triangle with base 14 cm and height 10 cm is: [2]

a) 24 cm²
b) 48 cm²
c) 70 cm² ✔
d) 140 cm²
Answer: c) 70 cm²
Area = ½ × base × height = ½ × 14 × 10 = 70 cm².

5. When a transversal cuts two parallel lines, the alternate interior angles are: [2]

a) Supplementary
b) Complementary
c) Equal ✔
d) None of these
Answer: c) Equal
When a transversal cuts two parallel lines, the alternate interior angles are always equal.

1. If 12 workers can build a wall in 15 days, how many days will 20 workers take to build the same wall? Also, if the wall needs to be built in 6 days, how many workers are needed? [4]

Answer:
This is an inverse proportion problem.
Total work = 12 workers × 15 days = 180 worker-days.

a) Days for 20 workers:
20 × days = 180
Days = 180 / 20
Days = 9 days

b) Workers needed for 6 days:
Workers × 6 = 180
Workers = 180 / 6
Workers = 30 workers

2. A trader buys 50 oranges for ₹500 and sells them at ₹8 each. Find:
a) The selling price of all oranges
b) The profit or loss
c) The profit or loss percentage [4]

Answer:
Cost Price (CP) = ₹500
CP per orange = 500 / 50 = ₹10
Selling Price per orange = ₹8

a) Selling price of all oranges:
SP = 50 × 8 = ₹400

b) Profit or Loss:
Since SP (₹400) < CP (₹500), there is a Loss.
Loss = CP − SP = 500 − 400 = ₹100

c) Loss percentage:
Loss% = (Loss / CP) × 100
= (100 / 500) × 100
= 20%

3. In triangle PQR and triangle XYZ: PQ = XY = 5 cm, QR = YZ = 7 cm, and PR = XZ = 6 cm.
a) Are the two triangles congruent? State the congruence criterion used.
b) Write the congruence statement.
c) If angle P = 55° and angle Q = 70°, find angle R, angle X, angle Y, and angle Z. [4]

Answer:

a) Yes, the two triangles are congruent by the SSS (Side-Side-Side) criterion.
PQ = XY = 5 cm, QR = YZ = 7 cm, PR = XZ = 6 cm.
All three pairs of corresponding sides are equal.

b) ▵PQR ≅ ▵XYZ

c) In ▵PQR:
Angle R = 180° − 55° − 70° = 55°

Since ▵PQR ≅ ▵XYZ, corresponding angles are equal:
Angle X = Angle P = 55°
Angle Y = Angle Q = 70°
Angle Z = Angle R = 55°

4. A rectangular plot is 40 m long and 30 m wide. A triangular garden with base 20 m and height 15 m is built inside the plot.
a) Find the area of the rectangular plot.
b) Find the area of the triangular garden.
c) Find the remaining area of the plot (outside the triangle).
d) If the remaining area is to be covered with grass costing ₹5 per m², find the total cost. [4]

Answer:

a) Area of the rectangular plot:
Area = length × width = 40 × 30 = 1200 m²

b) Area of the triangular garden:
Area = ½ × base × height = ½ × 20 × 15 = 150 m²

c) Remaining area:
Remaining area = 1200 − 150 = 1050 m²

d) Total cost of grass:
Cost = 1050 × 5 = ₹5,250
