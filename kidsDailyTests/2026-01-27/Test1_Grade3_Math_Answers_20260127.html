1. Which of these numbers is an odd number? [2]

a) 24
b) 38
c) 47 ✓
d) 52
Answer: c) 47
An odd number is a number that is not divisible by 2 (it does not end in 0, 2, 4, 6, or 8). 24, 38, and 52 all end in even digits, so they are even numbers. 47 ends in 7, which is an odd digit, so 47 is an odd number.

2. What is 503 − 268? [2]

a) 235 ✓
b) 245
c) 335
d) 345
Answer: a) 235
Step-by-step subtraction with borrowing:
Ones: 3 − 8 (cannot do, borrow from tens). But tens digit is 0, so borrow from hundreds.
Hundreds: 5 becomes 4, tens: 0 becomes 10, then borrow 1 to ones: tens becomes 9, ones: 13 − 8 = 5.
Tens: 9 − 6 = 3.
Hundreds: 4 − 2 = 2.
Result: 235.

3. A cube has how many faces? [2]

a) 4
b) 5
c) 6 ✓
d) 8
Answer: c) 6
A cube is a 3D shape with 6 square faces. It has a top face, a bottom face, a front face, a back face, a left face, and a right face. A cube also has 12 edges and 8 vertices (corners).

4. 3 kg 200 g = ___ grams [2]

a) 320
b) 3020
c) 3200 ✓
d) 32000
Answer: c) 3200
We know that 1 kg = 1000 g.
So 3 kg = 3 × 1000 = 3000 g.
3 kg 200 g = 3000 g + 200 g = 3200 g.

5. What comes next in the pattern: △, □, △, □, △, ___? [2]

a) △
b) □ ✓
c) ○
d) ☆
Answer: b) □
The pattern alternates between triangle (△) and square (□). The sequence is: △, □, △, □, △, so the next shape must be □ (square).

1. Compare using > , < or = : 4,897 ___ 4,978 [2]

Answer: 4,897 < 4,978
Step 1: Compare the thousands digit: both have 4 (same).
Step 2: Compare the hundreds digit: 8 vs 9. Since 8 < 9, we know 4,897 is less than 4,978.
Therefore, 4,897 < 4,978.

2. Reena stood 3rd in the race. Meena stood 7th. How many students finished between them? [2]

Answer: 3 students finished between them.
The positions between 3rd and 7th are: 4th, 5th, and 6th.
So 3 students finished between Reena and Meena.
(We do not count Reena or Meena themselves, only those in between.)

3. Divide 47 by 6. Write the quotient and remainder. [2]

Answer: 47 ÷ 6 = 7 remainder 5
Step 1: Find how many times 6 goes into 47. 6 × 7 = 42 (this is less than 47).
Step 2: 6 × 8 = 48 (this is more than 47, so 8 is too many).
Step 3: Quotient = 7.
Step 4: Remainder = 47 − 42 = 5.
So 47 ÷ 6 = 7 remainder 5.

4. A jug holds 2 litres of water. How many millilitres is that? If you pour out 750 ml, how much is left? [2]

Answer: 2 litres = 2000 ml. Water left = 1250 ml.
Step 1: Convert litres to millilitres. 1 litre = 1000 ml, so 2 litres = 2 × 1000 = 2000 ml.
Step 2: Pour out 750 ml. Water left = 2000 − 750 = 1250 ml.

5. In a survey, the tally marks for favourite fruits are: Apple: |||| |||| | , Banana: |||| |||, Mango: |||| |||| ||||. Which fruit is most liked and by how many children? [2]

Answer: Mango is the most liked fruit, chosen by 14 children.

Banana: |||| ||| = 5 + 3 = 8 children.
Mango: |||| |||| |||| = 5 + 5 + 4 = 14 children.

So Mango is the most liked fruit with 14 children choosing it.

1. In a school hall, chairs are arranged in 9 rows with 12 chairs in each row. 15 chairs are broken and removed. How many chairs are left? If 85 students sit down, how many empty chairs remain? [4]

Answer:
Step 1: Find the total number of chairs.
Total chairs = 9 rows × 12 chairs per row = 108 chairs.

Step 2: Remove the broken chairs.
Chairs left after removing broken ones = 108 − 15 = 93 chairs.

Step 3: Find how many empty chairs remain after students sit down.
Empty chairs = 93 − 85 = 8 empty chairs.

There are 93 chairs after removing the broken ones, and 8 empty chairs remain after 85 students sit down.

2. Arjun has three ₹100 notes, two ₹50 notes and five ₹10 coins. How much money does he have in total? He buys a book for ₹175 and a pen for ₹45. How much money is left? [4]

Answer:
Step 1: Calculate the total money Arjun has.
Three ₹100 notes = 3 × 100 = ₹300
Two ₹50 notes = 2 × 50 = ₹100
Five ₹10 coins = 5 × 10 = ₹50
Total money = 300 + 100 + 50 = ₹450

Step 2: Calculate the total amount spent.
Cost of book = ₹175
Cost of pen = ₹45
Total spent = 175 + 45 = ₹220

Step 3: Calculate money left.
Money left = 450 − 220 = ₹230

Arjun has ₹450 in total. After buying the book and pen, he has ₹230 left.

3. The bar graph shows the number of trees planted by four classes: Class A: 15 trees, Class B: 25 trees, Class C: 10 trees, Class D: 20 trees.

a) Which class planted the most trees?

b) How many more trees did Class B plant than Class C?

c) What is the total number of trees planted by all four classes? [4]

Answer:

a) Which class planted the most trees?
Class A = 15, Class B = 25, Class C = 10, Class D = 20.
Class B planted the most trees with 25 trees.

b) How many more trees did Class B plant than Class C?
Difference = Class B − Class C = 25 − 10 = 15 more trees.
Class B planted 15 more trees than Class C.

c) What is the total number of trees planted by all four classes?
Total = 15 + 25 + 10 + 20 = 70 trees.
All four classes planted a total of 70 trees.

4. Look at this pattern: 2, 6, 12, 20, 30, ___

a) What is the next number?

b) Explain the rule of this pattern.

c) What would be the 8th number in this pattern? [4]

Answer:

a) What is the next number?
The next number is 42.

b) Explain the rule of this pattern.
Find the differences between consecutive numbers:
6 − 2 = 4
12 − 6 = 6
20 − 12 = 8
30 − 20 = 10
The differences are: 4, 6, 8, 10, ... (increasing by 2 each time).
So the next difference = 12, and the next number = 30 + 12 = 42.

c) What would be the 8th number in this pattern?
Continue the pattern using the rule:
1st: 2
2nd: 6 (2 + 4)
3rd: 12 (6 + 6)
4th: 20 (12 + 8)
5th: 30 (20 + 10)
6th: 42 (30 + 12)
7th: 56 (42 + 14)
8th: 72 (56 + 16)

The 8th number in this pattern is 72.

5. A triangle has 3 sides and 3 angles. The angles of a triangle always add up to 180°.

a) If two angles of a triangle are 60° and 70°, find the third angle.

b) A rectangle has 4 right angles. What is each angle?

c) How many right angles are there in a square? Draw a square and mark all its right angles. [4]

Answer:

a) Find the third angle of the triangle.
The sum of all angles in a triangle = 180°.
First angle = 60°, Second angle = 70°.
Third angle = 180° − 60° − 70° = 180° − 130° = 50°.
The third angle is 50°.

b) What is each angle of a rectangle?
A rectangle has 4 right angles.
Each right angle = 90°.
So each angle of a rectangle is 90°.

c) How many right angles are there in a square?
A square has 4 right angles (each corner is a 90° angle).
The student should draw a square and mark a small square symbol at each of the 4 corners to indicate the right angles.
