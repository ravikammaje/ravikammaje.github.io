Grade 3 ICSE Science and Geography Test - Answer Key

Date: 31 January 2026

Total Marks: 40
Time: 25-30 minutes
Section A: Multiple Choice Questions (5 x 2 = 10 Marks)

1. Which sense organ helps us to hear sounds? Science

Answer: c) Ears

Ears are the sense organs for hearing.

2. What is the national bird of India? Geography

Answer: b) Peacock

The Indian Peacock is India's national bird.

3. Which part of a plant makes food? Science

Answer: c) Leaves

Leaves make food through photosynthesis using sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide.

4. The tricolor Indian flag has which color at the top? Geography

Answer: c) Saffron (Orange)

The Indian flag has saffron at the top, white in the middle, and green at the bottom.

5. How many bones does an adult human body have? Science

Answer: b) 206

An adult human has 206 bones (babies have about 270 which fuse together).

Section B: Short Answer Questions (5 x 2 = 10 Marks)

1. Name the five sense organs and what each helps us to do. Science [2]

Five sense organs:

1. Eyes - help us to see

2. Ears - help us to hear

3. Nose - helps us to smell

4. Tongue - helps us to taste

5. Skin - helps us to feel/touch

2. Name three neighboring countries of India. Geography [2]

Any three from: Pakistan, China, Nepal, Bhutan, Bangladesh, Myanmar, Sri Lanka

3. What are the functions of roots in a plant? Write any two. Science [2]

Functions of roots:

1. Roots absorb water and minerals from the soil

2. Roots hold the plant firmly in the ground (anchorage)

3. Some roots store food (e.g., carrot, radish)

4. Name the national animal, national flower, and national fruit of India. Geography [2]

National Animal: Tiger (Bengal Tiger)

National Flower: Lotus

National Fruit: Mango

5. What is a skeleton? Why do we need bones in our body? Science [2]

Skeleton: The framework of bones in our body is called the skeleton.

Why we need bones:

1. Bones give our body shape and support

2. Bones protect our internal organs (skull protects brain, ribs protect heart and lungs)

3. Bones help us move

Section C: Long Answer Questions (5 x 4 = 20 Marks)

1. Our Sense Organs: Science [4]

a) We use our nose to smell things. We should not smell unknown chemicals, gases, strong cleaning agents, or smoke as they can harm us.

b) Eyes help us to see colors, shapes, sizes, and movement. To protect eyes:

1. Do not read in dim light or very bright light

2. Do not watch TV or screens too closely or for too long

3. Wash eyes with clean water regularly

c) Our skin can feel: hot, cold, soft, hard, rough, smooth, pain, pressure (any four)

d) We should not put sharp objects in ears because:

- It can damage the eardrum

- It can cause hearing loss or infection

2. India - Our Country: Geography [4]

a) The capital of India is New Delhi. The Yamuna River flows through Delhi.

b) The highest mountain range in India is the Himalayas. It is located in the north of India.

c) Famous monuments of India:

1. Taj Mahal - Agra, Uttar Pradesh

2. Red Fort - Delhi

3. Gateway of India - Mumbai

4. Qutub Minar - Delhi (any two)

d) Indian Flag colors and meanings:

Saffron (top) - Courage and sacrifice

White (middle) - Peace and truth (with Ashoka Chakra in blue)

Green (bottom) - Fertility, growth, and prosperity

3. Parts of a Plant: Science [4]

a) Drawing should show a plant with labeled parts: roots (below ground), stem (main body), leaves (attached to stem), flower (at top/sides), and fruit (if present).

b) Functions of stem:

1. Holds the plant upright and supports branches, leaves, flowers

2. Carries water and minerals from roots to leaves

3. Carries food from leaves to other parts

c) Two types of roots:

1. Tap root - one main root with smaller side roots (e.g., carrot, radish, mango tree)

2. Fibrous root - many thin roots of similar size (e.g., grass, wheat, rice)

d) Plants need these to make food: Sunlight, Water, Carbon dioxide (and chlorophyll in leaves)

4. Environment and Pollution: Geography [4]

a) Environment is everything around us.

Natural things: Air, water, land, trees, plants, animals, rivers, mountains

Human-made things: Buildings, roads, bridges, cars, furniture, parks

b) Pollution is the addition of harmful substances to the environment that makes it dirty and unhealthy.

Three types: Air pollution, Water pollution, Land/Soil pollution

c) Ways to reduce air pollution:

1. Plant more trees

2. Use bicycles or walk for short distances

3. Use public transport instead of private vehicles

4. Avoid burning garbage

d) The 3 Rs are: Reduce, Reuse, Recycle

Reduce: Use less (save water, electricity, paper)

Reuse: Use things again instead of throwing away

Recycle: Make new things from old materials (paper, plastic, glass)

5. Human Skeletal System: Science [4]

a) The skull protects the brain from injury. It is made of hard bones that form a protective case around the brain.

b) The backbone (spine) is made up of 33 small bones called vertebrae. It protects the spinal cord which carries messages between the brain and the rest of the body.

c) Bones in arms and legs:

Arms: Humerus (upper arm), Radius and Ulna (forearm)

Legs: Femur (thigh bone), Tibia and Fibula (lower leg)

d) A joint is a place where two or more bones meet. Joints help us move our body parts.

Types of joints:

1. Ball and socket joint - shoulder, hip (allows movement in all directions)

2. Hinge joint - knee, elbow (allows back and forth movement like a door)

3. Pivot joint - neck (allows rotation)
