Section A: Multiple Choice Questions (5 x 2 = 10 Marks)

1. Sound travels fastest through: Physics

Answer: c) Steel

Sound travels fastest through solids, then liquids, then gases. It cannot travel through vacuum.

2. The number of protons in the nucleus of an atom is called: Chemistry

Answer: b) Atomic number

Atomic number = number of protons = number of electrons (in neutral atom)

3. The process by which plants make food using sunlight is called: Biology

Answer: c) Photosynthesis

Photo = light, synthesis = making. Plants make glucose using sunlight, CO₂, and water.

4. Heat transfer through a solid without the movement of particles is called: Physics

Answer: c) Conduction

In conduction, heat transfers by vibration of particles, not by their movement.

5. Which type of rock is formed by the cooling of molten magma? Geography

Answer: c) Igneous rock

Igneous rocks (e.g., granite, basalt) form when magma/lava cools and solidifies.

Section B: Short Answer Questions (5 x 2 = 10 Marks)

1. What is the frequency of a sound wave? What is its SI unit? Physics [2]

Frequency is the number of vibrations (oscillations) made by a vibrating body in one second.

SI unit: Hertz (Hz)

1 Hz = 1 vibration per second

2. Write the electronic configuration of: (i) Sodium (Na, atomic number 11) (ii) Chlorine (Cl, atomic number 17) Chemistry [2]

(i) Sodium (Na) - Atomic number 11:

Electronic configuration: 2, 8, 1

(ii) Chlorine (Cl) - Atomic number 17:

Electronic configuration: 2, 8, 7

3. Write the balanced equation for photosynthesis. Name the gas released during this process. Biology [2]

Balanced equation:

6CO₂ + 6H₂O → (sunlight + chlorophyll) → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂

Or: Carbon dioxide + Water → (sunlight + chlorophyll) → Glucose + Oxygen

Gas released: Oxygen (O₂)

4. What is a tissue? Name two types of plant tissues. Biology [2]

Tissue: A group of similar cells that perform a specific function together is called a tissue.

Two types of plant tissues:

1. Meristematic tissue - actively dividing cells (found at tips of roots and shoots)

2. Permanent tissue - cells that have stopped dividing (e.g., parenchyma, collenchyma, sclerenchyma)

5. What is weathering? Name any two agents of weathering. Geography [2]

Weathering: The breaking down and wearing away of rocks on the Earth's surface by natural agents is called weathering.

Two agents of weathering:

1. Water (rain, rivers, ice/glaciers)

2. Wind

Others: Temperature changes, plant roots, animals, chemical reactions

Section C: Long Answer Questions (5 x 4 = 20 Marks)

1. Sound: Physics [4]

a) Sound is a form of energy that produces the sensation of hearing. It is produced by vibrating objects. When an object vibrates, it causes the air particles around it to vibrate, creating a wave that travels to our ears.

b) Difference between loudness and pitch:

Loudness: How loud or soft a sound is. It depends on the amplitude of vibration. Greater amplitude = louder sound.

Pitch: How high or low a sound is. It depends on the frequency of vibration. Higher frequency = higher pitch.

c) Sound cannot travel through vacuum because sound needs a material medium (solid, liquid, or gas) to travel. Sound waves are mechanical waves that need particles to transmit vibrations. Vacuum has no particles, so sound cannot propagate through it.

d) Distance = Speed × Time

= 340 m/s × 5 s

= 1700 meters (or 1.7 km)

2. Atomic Structure: Chemistry [4]

a) Definitions:

Atom: The smallest particle of an element that can take part in a chemical reaction and retains all chemical properties of that element.

Molecule: The smallest particle of an element or compound that can exist independently and retains all properties of that substance.

Ion: An atom or group of atoms that has gained or lost electrons, carrying a positive or negative charge.

b) Oxygen atom (Z=8, A=16):

Protons = 8, Neutrons = 16-8 = 8, Electrons = 8

Electronic configuration: 2, 6

(Draw nucleus with 8p+8n, K shell with 2 electrons, L shell with 6 electrons)

c) Valency is the combining capacity of an atom, i.e., the number of electrons an atom can lose, gain, or share to achieve a stable configuration.

Magnesium (Z=12): Electronic configuration = 2, 8, 2

Valency = 2 (loses 2 electrons to achieve stable configuration)

d) Symbols and valencies:

Sodium: Na, valency 1

Calcium: Ca, valency 2

Chlorine: Cl, valency 1

Oxygen: O, valency 2

3. Photosynthesis: Biology [4]

a) Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants prepare their own food (glucose) using carbon dioxide, water, and sunlight in the presence of chlorophyll.

It occurs in the chloroplasts of plant cells, specifically in the green pigment called chlorophyll.

b) Raw materials for photosynthesis:

1. Carbon dioxide (CO₂) - from air through stomata

2. Water (H₂O) - absorbed by roots from soil

3. Sunlight - from the sun

4. Chlorophyll - present in chloroplasts

c) Factors affecting rate of photosynthesis:

1. Intensity of light

2. Concentration of carbon dioxide

3. Temperature

4. Availability of water

d) Leaves are green because they contain chlorophyll, which is a green pigment. Chlorophyll absorbs red and blue light and reflects green light, making leaves appear green.

Plants kept in dark: They cannot photosynthesize, so they turn yellow/pale (lose chlorophyll) and eventually die due to lack of food.

4. Heat Transfer: Physics [4]

a) Three modes of heat transfer:

1. Conduction: Transfer of heat through a substance without the movement of particles. Heat passes from particle to particle through vibration. Example: Metal spoon becomes hot when placed in hot soup.

2. Convection: Transfer of heat by actual movement of heated particles in fluids (liquids and gases). Example: Heating water in a pot - hot water rises, cold water sinks.

3. Radiation: Transfer of heat without any medium (can occur through vacuum). Example: Heat from the sun reaching Earth.

b) Cooking utensils are made of metal because metals are good conductors of heat - they allow heat to pass through quickly to cook food. Handles are made of wood or plastic because they are poor conductors (insulators) - they prevent heat from reaching our hands and burning them.

c) A thermos flask has:

- Double-walled glass container with vacuum between walls (prevents conduction and convection)

- Silvered inner surfaces (reflects radiant heat back)

- Insulated stopper (prevents heat loss from top)

This prevents all three modes of heat transfer, keeping liquids hot or cold.

d) White clothes in summer: White color reflects heat and keeps us cool.

Dark clothes in winter: Dark colors absorb heat and keep us warm.

5. Plant and Animal Tissues: Biology [4]

a) Difference between meristematic and permanent tissue:

Meristematic tissue:

- Cells are actively dividing

- Found at growing tips (root tips, shoot tips)

- Cells are small with thin walls

Permanent tissue:

- Cells have stopped dividing

- Found throughout the plant body

- Cells are differentiated for specific functions

b) Two types of permanent plant tissues:

1. Parenchyma: Thin-walled, living cells with large intercellular spaces. Function: Storage of food, photosynthesis, provides support.

2. Sclerenchyma: Thick-walled, dead cells. Function: Provides mechanical strength and rigidity to plant parts.

c) Epithelial tissue is a thin protective layer of cells that covers the body surfaces and lines the internal organs and cavities.

Found in: Skin, lining of mouth, food pipe, stomach, intestines, lungs, blood vessels.

d)

(i) Tissue that helps in movement: Muscular tissue

(ii) Tissue that carries messages: Nervous tissue
