Grade 3 ICSE Mathematics Test - Answer Key

Date: 31 January 2026

Total Marks: 40
Time: 25-30 minutes
Section A: Multiple Choice Questions (5 x 2 = 10 Marks)

1. What is 56 ÷ 8?

Answer: b) 7

56 ÷ 8 = 7 (8 × 7 = 56)

2. How many minutes are there in 2 hours?

Answer: d) 120 minutes

1 hour = 60 minutes, so 2 hours = 2 × 60 = 120 minutes

3. Which 3D shape has 6 faces, 12 edges, and 8 vertices?

Answer: c) Cuboid

A cuboid (and cube) has 6 faces, 12 edges, and 8 vertices

4. Which of these is an odd number?

Answer: c) 47

47 ends in 7, which makes it odd. Others end in 4, 6, 8 (even)

5. In a pictograph, if one symbol represents 5 books, how many books do 4 symbols show?

Answer: c) 20 books

4 × 5 = 20 books

Section B: Short Answer Questions (5 x 2 = 10 Marks)

1. Divide 72 by 9. Then multiply the answer by 4. What do you get? [2]

72 ÷ 9 = 8

8 × 4 = 32

2. Riya's school starts at 8:30 a.m. and ends at 2:15 p.m. How long is her school day? [2]

From 8:30 a.m. to 2:30 p.m. = 6 hours

From 2:30 p.m. back to 2:15 p.m. = subtract 15 minutes

School day = 5 hours 45 minutes

3. Draw a cube. How many faces does it have? Are all faces the same shape? [2]

A cube has 6 faces

Yes, all faces are the same shape - they are all squares of equal size

4. Write all the even numbers between 41 and 55. [2]

42, 44, 46, 48, 50, 52, 54

5. Look at this tally chart: Apples: |||| |||| | , Oranges: |||| |||. How many more apples than oranges are there? [2]

Apples: |||| |||| | = 5 + 5 + 1 = 11

Oranges: |||| ||| = 5 + 3 = 8

Difference: 11 - 8 = 3 more apples

Section C: Long Answer Questions (5 x 4 = 20 Marks)

1. Division Problems: [4]

a) 84 ÷ 7 = 12 mangoes in each basket

b) 96 ÷ 8 = 12 students in each row

c) 108 ÷ 12 = 9

Long division: 12 goes into 108 nine times (12 × 9 = 108)

d) 63 ÷ 9 = 7 cm each piece

2. Time and Money: [4]

a) 4:45 p.m. + 1 hour 50 minutes

4:45 + 1 hour = 5:45 p.m.

5:45 + 50 minutes = 6:35 p.m.

b) Rs 100 - Rs 35 - Rs 18 = Rs 100 - Rs 53 = Rs 47

c) 3 hours = 3 × 60 = 180 minutes

180 + 25 = 205 minutes

d) 96 ÷ 8 = 12 pencils

3. 3D Shapes: [4]

a) Objects shaped like a cylinder: Water bottle, can, pipe, rolling pin, drum (any two)

b) Differences between cube and cuboid:

1. A cube has all equal square faces; a cuboid has rectangular faces

2. A cube has all equal edges; a cuboid has edges of different lengths

c) A cone has: 2 faces (1 flat circular base + 1 curved surface), 1 vertex (the tip), 1 edge (the circular edge)

d) Sphere has no edges and no vertices. Example: ball, globe

4. Odd and Even Numbers: [4]

a) Odd numbers between 60 and 80: 61, 63, 65, 67, 69, 71, 73, 75, 77, 79

b) When you add two even numbers, the answer is even

Example: 4 + 6 = 10 (even)

c) When you add an odd and an even number, the answer is odd

Example: 5 + 4 = 9 (odd)

d) Odd numbers: 23, 67, 15, 39

Even numbers: 48, 90, 82, 56

5. Data Handling (Pictograph): [4]

Each symbol = 3 students

a) Mango: 4 symbols × 3 = 12 students

b) Apple is liked by the most students (5 symbols)

5 × 3 = 15 students

c) Apple: 15 students, Grapes: 2 × 3 = 6 students

15 - 6 = 9 more students like Apple than Grapes

d) Total = Mango + Banana + Apple + Grapes

= 12 + 9 + 15 + 6 = 42 students
