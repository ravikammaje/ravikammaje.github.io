Section B: Short Answer Solutions
1. Physical vs Chemical change:
  ‚Ä¢ Physical change: No new substance formed, reversible (e.g., melting ice, dissolving salt)
  ‚Ä¢ Chemical change: New substance formed, usually irreversible (e.g., burning paper, rusting)
2. Heat transfer:
  (a) Conduction: Heat transfer through direct contact, molecule to molecule (in solids)
  (b) Convection: Heat transfer through fluid movement (liquids/gases)
  (c) Radiation: Heat transfer through electromagnetic waves, no medium needed
3. Digestive system:
  (a) Salivary glands: Produce saliva containing amylase to digest starch
  (b) Small intestine: Complete digestion and absorption of nutrients into blood
  (c) Large intestine: Absorbs water and forms solid waste (feces)
4. Acid-base:
  (a) Acidic (turns blue litmus red, pH < 7)
  (b) Neutralization occurs, pH increases toward 7
  (c) Vinegar, lemon juice, or HCl (dilute)
5. Lightning before thunder: Light travels much faster (3√ó10‚Å∏ m/s) than sound (330 m/s). So light from lightning reaches us almost instantly, while sound takes longer to travel the same distance.
