11. Respiration in Humans:
a) Organs involved: Nose, pharynx, larynx, trachea (windpipe), bronchi, lungs, diaphragm
b) Inhalation: Diaphragm contracts and moves down, rib cage expands, air pressure decreases in lungs, air rushes in
Exhalation: Diaphragm relaxes and moves up, rib cage contracts, air pressure increases, air is pushed out
c) Gas exchange: Occurs in alveoli (air sacs) in lungs; oxygen diffuses into blood, carbon dioxide diffuses from blood into alveoli to be exhaled

13. Ecosystem:
An ecosystem is a community of living organisms (biotic) interacting with each other and their non-living environment (abiotic factors like water, air, soil, sunlight).
‚Ä¢ Producer: Organisms that make their own food through photosynthesis. Examples: plants, algae, phytoplankton
‚Ä¢ Consumer: Organisms that cannot make their own food and eat other organisms. Examples: herbivores (deer, rabbit), carnivores (lion, eagle), omnivores (bear, humans)
‚Ä¢ Decomposer: Organisms that break down dead organic matter and return nutrients to soil. Examples: bacteria, fungi, earthworms
14. Water Cycle:
The continuous movement of water on, above, and below Earth's surface.
‚Ä¢ Evaporation: Sun heats water in oceans/lakes/rivers, turning it into water vapor that rises
‚Ä¢ Condensation: Water vapor cools in the atmosphere and forms clouds (tiny water droplets)
‚Ä¢ Precipitation: Water falls back to Earth as rain, snow, sleet, or hail
‚Ä¢ Collection: Water collects in oceans, lakes, rivers, or seeps into ground (groundwater)
Diagram should show: sun, ocean/water body, clouds, arrows showing evaporation going up, precipitation coming down, water flowing back to ocean
15. Heat Transfer - Conductors and Insulators:
‚Ä¢ Metal spoon: Metals are good conductors of heat. They have free electrons that can move and transfer thermal energy quickly from particle to particle. Heat from hot water transfers rapidly through the metal to your hand.
‚Ä¢ Wooden spoon: Wood is a poor conductor (insulator). It does not have free electrons, so heat transfer is very slow. The particles in wood do not easily pass thermal energy along.
‚Ä¢ Conclusion: Materials differ in their ability to conduct heat. Conductors (metals) transfer heat quickly; insulators (wood, plastic, rubber) transfer heat slowly, which is why they feel cooler to touch.
