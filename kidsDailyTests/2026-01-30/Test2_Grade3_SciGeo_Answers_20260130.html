Grade 3 ICSE Science and Geography Test - Answer Key

Date: 30 January 2026

Total Marks: 40
Time: 25-30 minutes
Section A: Multiple Choice Questions (5 x 2 = 10 Marks)

1. Which state of matter has a fixed shape and volume? Science

Answer: c) Solid

Solids have definite shape and volume.

2. How many continents are there on Earth? Geography

Answer: c) 7

The 7 continents are Asia, Africa, North America, South America, Antarctica, Europe, and Australia.

3. What happens to water when it is heated strongly? Science

Answer: b) It becomes steam

Water evaporates and turns into water vapor/steam when heated.

4. Which is the largest ocean in the world? Geography

Answer: c) Pacific Ocean

Pacific Ocean covers about one-third of Earth's surface.

5. Which of the following is NOT a property of water? Science

Answer: c) Sweet tasting

Water is tasteless, not sweet. It is colorless and odorless.

Section B: Short Answer Questions (5 x 2 = 10 Marks)

1. Name the three states of matter and give one example of each. Science [2]

Answer:

1. Solid - Example: Ice, stone, wood, book

2. Liquid - Example: Water, milk, juice, oil

3. Gas - Example: Air, oxygen, carbon dioxide, steam

2. Name any four continents and one country in each. Geography [2]

Answer:

1. Asia - India (or China, Japan)

2. Africa - Egypt (or South Africa, Kenya)

3. Europe - France (or Germany, UK)

4. North America - USA (or Canada, Mexico)

3. What is the water cycle? Name two stages of the water cycle. Science [2]

Answer:

The water cycle is the continuous movement of water from Earth's surface to the atmosphere and back again.

Two stages: Evaporation and Condensation (or Precipitation)

4. Name the four cardinal directions. In which direction does the Sun rise? Geography [2]

Answer:

The four cardinal directions are: North, South, East, West

The Sun rises in the East.

5. What is dissolved in seawater that makes it salty? Can you drink seawater directly? Science [2]

Answer:

Salt (sodium chloride) is dissolved in seawater.

No, we cannot drink seawater directly because it contains too much salt, which can make us sick and cause dehydration.

Section C: Long Answer Questions (5 x 4 = 20 Marks)

1. States of Matter: Science [4]

Answers:

a) Three states of matter: Solid, Liquid, and Gas

b) Liquids can be poured because they have no fixed shape - they take the shape of their container and their particles can flow past each other.

c) Examples from home:

Solids: Table, chair, book, spoon, plate

Liquids: Water, milk, cooking oil, shampoo, juice

Gases: Air, cooking gas (LPG), perfume spray, steam from cooking

d) Ice melts and becomes water. This change is called melting. It happens because heat from the environment warms the ice.

2. Our Earth - Continents and Oceans: Geography [4]

Answers:

a) 7 Continents: Asia, Africa, North America, South America, Antarctica, Europe, Australia (Oceania)

b) 5 Oceans: Pacific Ocean, Atlantic Ocean, Indian Ocean, Arctic Ocean, Southern Ocean (Antarctic Ocean)

c) Antarctica is known as the "frozen continent" because it is covered with thick ice and snow throughout the year. It is the coldest place on Earth.

d) India is located in Asia. Neighboring countries include: Pakistan, China, Nepal, Bhutan, Bangladesh, Myanmar, Sri Lanka (any two)

3. Water - A Precious Resource: Science [4]

Answers:

a) Three sources of water: Rivers, Lakes, Groundwater (wells), Rain, Oceans, Glaciers (any three)

b) Water Cycle Diagram should show:

- Evaporation: Water from oceans/rivers turns into water vapor due to Sun's heat

- Condensation: Water vapor cools and forms clouds

- Precipitation: Water falls back as rain, snow, or hail

c) Importance of conserving water:

- Fresh water is limited on Earth

- Many people don't have access to clean water

- Water is needed for drinking, farming, and daily activities

d) Ways to save water at home:

- Turn off tap while brushing teeth

- Fix leaking taps

- Take shorter showers

- Use water from washing vegetables to water plants

4. The Globe and Directions: Geography [4]

Answers:

a) A globe is a spherical model of the Earth. It represents the shape of Earth and shows continents, oceans, and countries in their correct positions.

b) We have day and night because Earth rotates (spins) on its axis. The side facing the Sun has day, and the side away from the Sun has night. Earth takes 24 hours to complete one rotation.

c) The four main directions are called Cardinal Directions: North (N), South (S), East (E), West (W). A compass rose shows these directions with North at the top.

d) If you face the rising Sun (East), your left side will face North.

5. Changes in States of Matter: Science [4]

Answers:

a) Melting is the change from solid to liquid state when heat is added. Example: Ice melting into water, butter melting when heated.

b) Evaporation is the change from liquid to gas state. Example: Water drying up from wet roads, puddles disappearing on a sunny day.

c) Freezing is the change from liquid to solid state when cooled. Water freezes at 0 degrees Celsius (or 32 degrees Fahrenheit).

d) Wet clothes dry when hung in the sun because the water in the clothes evaporates. The heat from the sun gives energy to water molecules, causing them to escape into the air as water vapor. This process is called evaporation.
