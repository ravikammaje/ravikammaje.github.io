Section C: Long Answer Questions (5 x 4 = 20 Marks)

Answer the following questions in detail.

1. States of Matter: Science [4]

a) What are the three states of matter?

b) Which state of matter can be poured? Why?

c) Give two examples each of solids, liquids, and gases found in your home.

d) What happens to ice when you leave it outside on a hot day? What is this change called?

2. Our Earth - Continents and Oceans: Geography [4]

a) Name all 7 continents of the world.

b) Name all 5 oceans of the world.

c) Which continent is known as the "frozen continent"? Why?

d) India is located in which continent? Name two neighboring countries of India.

3. Water - A Precious Resource: Science [4]

a) Name three sources of water.

b) Draw and label a simple diagram of the water cycle showing: evaporation, condensation, and precipitation.

c) Why is it important to conserve water? Give two reasons.

d) Write two ways you can save water at home.

4. The Globe and Directions: Geography [4]

a) What is a globe? What does it represent?

b) Why do we have day and night? (Hint: Think about Earth's rotation)

c) What are the four main directions called? Draw a compass rose showing all four directions.

d) If you face the rising Sun, which direction will be on your left?

5. Changes in States of Matter: Science [4]

a) What is melting? Give an example.

b) What is evaporation? Give an example from daily life.

c) What is freezing? At what temperature does water freeze?

d) Explain why wet clothes dry when hung in the sun. Which process is involved?
