11. Types of leaf venation:

1. Reticulate Venation (Net-like pattern):
- Veins form a network or web-like pattern
- Example: Mango, Peepal, Banyan, Rose

2. Parallel Venation:
- Veins run parallel to each other
- Example: Grass, Wheat, Banana, Coconut

Award marks: Definition of each type (2 marks), Examples (2 marks)

13. Parts of a Flower and their functions:

Students should draw a flower and label these parts:

1. Petals: Colorful parts that attract insects and birds for pollination
2. Sepals: Green leaf-like parts that protect the flower bud
3. Stamens (Male part): Produce pollen grains
4. Pistil/Carpel (Female part): Receives pollen and produces seeds
5. Stem (Pedicel): Connects flower to the branch

Award marks: Correct diagram (1 mark), Four labeled parts (2 marks), Functions described (1 mark)
