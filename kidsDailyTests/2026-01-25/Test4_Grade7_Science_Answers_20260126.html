6. Physics State the two laws of reflection of light.
Answer:
First Law: The incident ray, the reflected ray, and the normal at the point of incidence all lie in the same plane.

Second Law: The angle of incidence is equal to the angle of reflection.
(∠i = ∠r)
7. Chemistry What is corrosion?
Answer:
Corrosion is the slow destruction of metals due to chemical reactions with substances in the environment (like oxygen, water, acids).

Example: Rusting of iron (when iron reacts with oxygen and moisture to form iron oxide - rust)

Prevention methods:
- Painting or coating the metal surface
- Galvanization (coating with zinc)
- Oiling or greasing
- Electroplating
8. Biology Name the two main types of plant tissues.
Answer:
1. Meristematic tissue:
- Actively dividing cells
- Found at tips of roots and shoots
- Example: Apical meristem

2. Permanent tissue:
- Cells that have stopped dividing
- Differentiated for specific functions
- Examples: Xylem, Phloem, Parenchyma, Collenchyma, Sclerenchyma

10. Physics Three characteristics of sound
Answer:
The three characteristics of sound are:

1. Loudness - depends on amplitude of sound waves (measured in decibels)

2. Pitch - depends on frequency of sound waves (high or low sound)

3. Quality (Timbre) - helps distinguish between different sources of sound

Quality/Timbre helps us identify different voices and musical instruments.

Section C: Long Answer Questions (5 × 4 = 20 Marks)

11. Biology Human excretory system
Answer:
a) Main organs of excretory system:
- Two Kidneys
- Two Ureters
- Urinary bladder
- Urethra

b) Function of kidneys:
- Filter blood to remove waste products
- Maintain water and salt balance in body
- Remove urea, excess salts, and water
- Produce urine
- Each kidney contains millions of nephrons (filtering units)

c) Role of urinary bladder:
- Stores urine temporarily
- Can hold about 300-400 mL of urine
- Muscular sac that contracts to expel urine

d) Two waste products removed:
1. Urea (from protein breakdown)
2. Excess water and salts
(Also: creatinine, uric acid)
12. Physics Working of an electromagnet
Answer:
a) Materials needed:
- Soft iron core (nail or rod)
- Insulated copper wire
- Battery/power source
- Switch (optional)

b) How to increase strength:
- Increase the number of turns of wire
- Increase the current flowing through the wire
- Use a soft iron core (instead of air core)

c) Uses of electromagnets:
1. Electric bells and buzzers
2. Cranes in scrapyards to lift heavy iron/steel
3. MRI machines in hospitals
4. Electric motors and generators

d) Difference from permanent magnet:
- Electromagnet: Magnetism can be turned on/off; strength can be controlled
- Permanent magnet: Always magnetic; strength is fixed; made of hard magnetic materials

PropertyMetalsNon-metals
StateUsually solid (except mercury)Can be solid, liquid, or gas
LustreShinyDull (except diamond, iodine)
ConductivityGood conductors of heat and electricityPoor conductors (except graphite)
MalleabilityCan be beaten into sheetsBrittle, cannot be beaten

b) Examples:
Metals: Iron (Fe), Copper (Cu), Gold (Au), Aluminium (Al)
Non-metals: Carbon (C), Sulphur (S), Oxygen (O), Nitrogen (N)

c) Metalloid:
Example: Silicon (Si) or Boron
Called metalloid because it has properties of both metals and non-metals. Silicon looks shiny like metal but is brittle like non-metal; it's a semiconductor (conducts electricity partially).
14. Biology Five Kingdom Classification
Answer:
The Five Kingdoms are:

1. Kingdom Monera:
Characteristics: Unicellular, prokaryotic (no nucleus), microscopic
Example: Bacteria, Blue-green algae (Cyanobacteria)

2. Kingdom Protista:
Characteristics: Unicellular, eukaryotic (has nucleus), can be autotrophic or heterotrophic
Example: Amoeba, Paramecium, Euglena

3. Kingdom Fungi:
Characteristics: Eukaryotic, heterotrophic, cell wall made of chitin, decomposers
Example: Mushroom, Yeast, Bread mold

4. Kingdom Plantae:
Characteristics: Multicellular, eukaryotic, autotrophic (make own food by photosynthesis), cell wall made of cellulose
Example: Rose plant, Mango tree, Fern

5. Kingdom Animalia:
Characteristics: Multicellular, eukaryotic, heterotrophic, no cell wall
Example: Human, Tiger, Butterfly
15. Geography Global Warming
Answer:
a) What is global warming?
Global warming is the gradual increase in Earth's average surface temperature due to the enhanced greenhouse effect caused by human activities.

b) Greenhouse effect:
- Sun's rays enter Earth's atmosphere and warm the surface
- Earth radiates heat back as infrared radiation
- Greenhouse gases trap this heat, preventing it from escaping
- This keeps Earth warm (natural process)
- Too many greenhouse gases = too much heat trapped = global warming

c) Three greenhouse gases:
1. Carbon dioxide (CO₂)
2. Methane (CH₄)
3. Nitrous oxide (N₂O)
(Also: Water vapor, CFCs)

d) Effects of global warming:
1. Melting of polar ice caps and glaciers
2. Rise in sea levels (flooding coastal areas)
3. Extreme weather events (storms, droughts)
4. Changes in rainfall patterns

Ways to reduce global warming:
1. Reduce use of fossil fuels (use renewable energy)
2. Plant more trees (afforestation)
3. Reduce, reuse, recycle
4. Use public transport or electric vehicles
