Grade 3 ICSE Mathematics Test

ANSWER KEY - January 26, 2026

Section A: Multiple Choice Questions (5 x 2 = 10 Marks)

1. A cylinder has how many curved surfaces?
Answer: b) 1
(A cylinder has 1 curved surface and 2 flat circular faces)
2. Which of these numbers is divisible by 5?
Answer: c) 385
(Numbers ending in 0 or 5 are divisible by 5)
3. What is 12 x 7?
Answer: b) 84
4. 3 litres = ___ millilitres
Answer: c) 3000 mL
(1 litre = 1000 mL, so 3 L = 3000 mL)
5. Which sport has the tallest bar?
Answer: a) Cricket
(Cricket has 15, which is the highest)

Section B: Short Answer Questions (5 x 2 = 10 Marks)

6. What is the next number in this pattern? 5, 10, 20, 40, ___
Answer:
Pattern rule: Each number is doubled (multiply by 2)
5 x 2 = 10
10 x 2 = 20
20 x 2 = 40
40 x 2 = 80
Answer: 80
7. A bucket holds 8 litres of water. How many 500 mL bottles can be filled?
Answer:
8 litres = 8000 mL
Number of bottles = 8000 / 500 = 16 bottles
8. Write the place value of 4 in the number 4,827.
Answer:
In 4,827:
4 is in the thousands place
Place value of 4 = 4,000 (Four Thousand)
9. Find: 96 divided by 12. Write one multiplication fact.
Answer:
96 / 12 = 8
Multiplication fact: 12 x 8 = 96 (or 8 x 12 = 96)
10. Name a 3D shape with 1 flat face and 1 curved surface.
Answer:
Shape: Cone
Number of vertices: 1 (the pointed tip at the top)

Section C: Long Answer Questions (5 x 4 = 20 Marks)

11. Tally chart problem - How students travel to school
Answer:
Bus: 12 students
Car: 8 students
Walk: 15 students
Bicycle: 6 students

a) Total students = 12 + 8 + 15 + 6 = 41 students

b) Most common way = Walk (15 students)

c) Walk - Car = 15 - 8 = 7 more students

d) Current cyclists + 5 = 6 + 5 = 11 students
12. Water tank problem
Answer:
Tank capacity = 25 litres
Morning usage = 8 L 500 mL
Afternoon usage = 6 L 750 mL

a) Total water used:
8 L 500 mL + 6 L 750 mL
= 14 L 1250 mL
= 15 L 250 mL (since 1250 mL = 1 L 250 mL)

b) Water left:
25 L - 15 L 250 mL
= 24 L 1000 mL - 15 L 250 mL
= 9 L 750 mL

c) In millilitres:
9 L 750 mL = 9000 mL + 750 mL = 9750 mL
13. Arranging numbers and operations
Answer:
Numbers: 6,543 ; 6,453 ; 6,534 ; 6,345

a) Descending order:
6,543 > 6,534 > 6,453 > 6,345

b) Difference between largest and smallest:
Largest = 6,543
Smallest = 6,345
Difference = 6,543 - 6,345 = 198

c) Round 6,543 to nearest hundred:
Look at tens digit: 4 (less than 5, so round down)
Answer: 6,500
14. 2D shapes - sides and symmetry
Answer:
a) Number of sides:
Square: 4 sides
Triangle: 3 sides
Pentagon: 5 sides
Hexagon: 6 sides

b) Most lines of symmetry:
Square: 4 lines of symmetry
Equilateral Triangle: 3 lines
Regular Pentagon: 5 lines
Regular Hexagon: 6 lines
Answer: Hexagon has the most (6 lines of symmetry)

c) Shape with exactly 3 lines of symmetry:
Equilateral Triangle (all sides equal, all angles equal to 60 degrees)
15. Pencil box problem - division and money
Answer:
Total pencils = 168
Pencils per box = 12

a) Number of boxes:
168 / 12 = 14 boxes

b) Total cost of boxes:
14 boxes x Rs 5 = Rs 70

c) Money earned from selling:
14 boxes x Rs 50 = Rs 700

d) Profit:
Selling price - Cost of boxes = Rs 700 - Rs 70 = Rs 630
(Note: If considering only the box cost, profit = Rs 630. If the pencils had a purchase cost, that would also need to be subtracted.)
