Section C: Long Answer Questions (5 x 4 = 20 Marks)

11. Science States of matter and changes
Answer:
Three states of matter:
1. Solid - Has fixed shape and volume (e.g., ice, rock)
2. Liquid - Has fixed volume but takes shape of container (e.g., water, milk)
3. Gas - Has no fixed shape or volume (e.g., air, steam)

a) When ice is heated:
Ice (solid) changes to Water (liquid). This is called melting.

b) When water is boiled:
Water (liquid) changes to Steam (gas). This is called evaporation or boiling.

c) This process is called:
Change of state - Matter changes from one state to another when heated or cooled.
12. Geography Seven continents
Answer:
The seven continents are:
1. Asia
2. Africa
3. North America
4. South America
5. Antarctica
6. Europe
7. Australia (Oceania)

Largest continent: Asia - It is the biggest in both area and population.

Frozen continent: Antarctica
It is called the frozen continent because:
- It is covered with ice and snow all year round
- It is the coldest place on Earth
- No people live there permanently (only scientists)
- It is located at the South Pole
13. Science Human skeletal system
Answer:
a) Functions of the skeleton:
- Gives shape and support to our body
- Protects internal organs
- Helps us move (along with muscles)
- Produces blood cells (in bone marrow)

b) Bone that protects our brain:
Skull (cranium) - It is a hard, bony case that covers and protects the brain.

c) Bones that protect heart and lungs:
Rib cage - It is made up of 12 pairs of ribs that form a protective cage around the heart and lungs.

d) Longest bone in the body:
Femur (thigh bone) - It is the bone in our upper leg, connecting the hip to the knee.
14. Geography Pollution and environment care
Answer:
a) Three types of pollution:
1. Air pollution
2. Water pollution
3. Land (soil) pollution

b) Examples of each type:
- Air pollution: Smoke from vehicles and factories
- Water pollution: Throwing garbage and chemicals in rivers
- Land pollution: Plastic waste, garbage dumps

c) The 3R concept:
Reduce - Use less, buy only what we need
Reuse - Use things again instead of throwing away
Recycle - Convert waste into new products

d) Ways to reduce pollution:
- Use less plastic, carry cloth bags
- Plant more trees
- Use bicycle or walk for short distances
- Don't throw garbage in water bodies
- Turn off lights and fans when not in use
15. Science Plants and their parts
Answer:
a) Two types of roots with examples:
1. Tap root - One main root grows deep (e.g., Carrot, Radish, Mango tree)
2. Fibrous root - Many thin roots spread out (e.g., Grass, Wheat, Rice)

b) Function of leaves:
- Make food for the plant through photosynthesis
- Leaves use sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide to make food (glucose)
- Release oxygen into the air
- Help in transpiration (losing excess water)

c) Foods from roots:
1. Carrot
2. Radish
3. Beetroot
4. Turnip
5. Sweet potato

d) Foods from stems:
1. Potato (underground stem)
2. Sugarcane
3. Ginger
4. Onion (bulb - modified stem)
5. Turmeric
