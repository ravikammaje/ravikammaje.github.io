Grade 3 ICSE Science and Geography Test - ANSWER KEY

Date: 28 January 2026

Total Marks: 40
Time: 25-30 minutes
Section A: Multiple Choice Questions (5 × 2 = 10 Marks)

1. Which is a living thing? Science

Answer: c) Butterfly

Butterfly can move, breathe, grow, and reproduce

2. How many continents? Geography

Answer: c) 7

Asia, Africa, North America, South America, Antarctica, Europe, Australia

3. How many legs does an insect have? Science

Answer: b) 6

All insects have 6 legs (3 pairs)

4. National bird of India? Geography

Answer: b) Peacock

Peacock (Indian Peafowl) was declared national bird in 1963

5. Water changes to vapor when: Science

Answer: b) Heated

Heat causes evaporation - water turns to water vapor

Section B: Short Answer Questions (5 × 2 = 10 Marks)

1. Three differences between living and non-living things: Science

Differences:

1. Living things breathe; non-living things do not breathe

2. Living things grow; non-living things do not grow

3. Living things reproduce; non-living things cannot reproduce

(Also: Living things need food, can move on their own, respond to stimuli)

2. Name the five oceans: Geography

The five oceans are:

1. Pacific Ocean (largest)

2. Atlantic Ocean

3. Indian Ocean

4. Southern Ocean (Antarctic Ocean)

5. Arctic Ocean (smallest)

3. Three parts of an insect's body and examples: Science

Three body parts: Head, Thorax, Abdomen

Examples of insects: Butterfly, Ant, Bee, Grasshopper, Mosquito

(Any two examples acceptable)

4. Four cardinal directions and sunrise: Geography

Four cardinal directions: North, South, East, West

The sun rises from the East

5. Three states of water with examples: Science

Three states of water:

1. Solid - Ice, snow, hailstones

2. Liquid - Water in rivers, lakes, tap water

3. Gas - Water vapor, steam

Section C: Long Answer Questions (5 × 4 = 20 Marks)

1. Characteristics of living things: Science

a) Four characteristics of living things:

1. They breathe (respiration)

2. They need food (nutrition)

3. They grow

4. They reproduce (have babies)

(Also acceptable: movement, excretion, respond to stimuli)

b) Is a car a living thing?

No, a car is NOT a living thing because:

- It does not breathe

- It does not grow

- It cannot reproduce

- It cannot move on its own (needs a driver)

c) Examples from a garden:

Living: Plants, flowers, butterflies, ants, birds, earthworms

Non-living: Stones, soil, water, pots, fences, benches

2. Continents and oceans: Geography

a) Seven continents:

Asia, Africa, North America, South America, Antarctica, Europe, Australia (Oceania)

b) Largest and smallest:

Largest continent: Asia

Smallest continent: Australia

c) We live in:

Asia (India is in Asia)

d) Ocean south of India:

Indian Ocean

3. Insects: Science

a) Three body parts of an insect:

Head (eyes, antennae, mouth) - Thorax (legs, wings) - Abdomen

(Drawing should show these three distinct parts)

b) Two useful insects:

1. Bee - makes honey, helps in pollination

2. Silk worm - produces silk

(Also: Ladybug eats harmful pests, Butterfly helps pollination)

c) Two harmful insects:

1. Mosquito - spreads diseases like malaria, dengue

2. Locust - destroys crops

(Also: Housefly spreads germs, Cockroach spreads diseases)

d) Life cycle of butterfly (4 stages):

1. Egg → 2. Larva (Caterpillar) → 3. Pupa (Chrysalis) → 4. Adult Butterfly

4. National symbols of India: Geography

a) National animal:

Bengal Tiger (Royal Bengal Tiger)

b) National flower:

Lotus

c) Indian national flag:

The flag has three horizontal stripes:

- Saffron (top) - represents courage and sacrifice

- White (middle) - represents peace and truth, has the Ashoka Chakra (wheel with 24 spokes)

- Green (bottom) - represents fertility and prosperity

d) National emblem:

The Lion Capital of Ashoka (Ashoka Stambh)

Seen on: Government documents, currency notes, passports, official letterheads

5. Water cycle: Science

a) Evaporation:

Evaporation is the process by which water changes from liquid to gas (water vapor) when heated by the sun.

The water vapor rises up into the atmosphere (sky).

b) Condensation:

Condensation is the process by which water vapor cools and changes back into tiny water droplets.

Clouds form when water vapor rises high into the sky, cools down, and condenses into tiny water droplets that gather together.

c) Precipitation:

Precipitation is when water falls from clouds back to Earth.

Forms of precipitation: Rain, Snow, Hail, Sleet

d) Importance of water cycle:

The water cycle is important because:

- It provides fresh water for drinking

- It helps plants grow (rain waters plants)

- It fills rivers, lakes, and groundwater

- It keeps the Earth's temperature balanced
