Section C: Long Answer Questions (5 × 4 = 20 Marks)

Answer the following questions in detail.

1. Light and Reflection: Physics [4]

a) Draw a diagram showing the reflection of light from a plane mirror. Label the incident ray, reflected ray, normal, angle of incidence, and angle of reflection.

b) If the angle of incidence is 35°, what will be the angle of reflection?

c) Why can we see ourselves in a plane mirror but not in a rough wall?

d) What is the speed of light in vacuum?

2. Physical and Chemical Changes: Chemistry [4]

a) Classify the following as physical or chemical change: (i) Rusting of iron (ii) Boiling of water (iii) Digestion of food (iv) Cutting of wood

b) What happens when you heat sugar until it turns black? Is this a physical or chemical change? Explain.

c) Can a chemical change be reversed? Give a reason with an example.

d) Name two indicators of a chemical change.

3. Photosynthesis: Biology [4]

a) What is photosynthesis? Write the balanced chemical equation for photosynthesis.

b) List the four factors necessary for photosynthesis to occur.

c) Where in the leaf does photosynthesis mainly take place? Why?

d) Why is photosynthesis important for life on Earth? Give two reasons.

4. Metals and Non-Metals: Chemistry [4]

a) Name three metals that are good conductors of electricity. Which metal is the best conductor?

b) What is corrosion? Give an example of corrosion and how it can be prevented.

c) Why are cooking utensils made of metals like aluminum and steel?

d) Name two non-metals that are solid at room temperature and one that is a gas. Which non-metal is essential for breathing?

5. The Atmosphere and Global Warming: Geography [4]

a) Name the five layers of Earth's atmosphere in order from lowest to highest. Which layer contains the ozone layer?

b) What is the greenhouse effect? Name two greenhouse gases.

c) What is global warming? List two causes and two effects of global warming.

d) Suggest two ways to reduce global warming.
