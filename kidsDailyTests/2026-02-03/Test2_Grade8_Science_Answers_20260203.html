Right Atrium - receives deoxygenated blood from the body 2. Right Ventricle - pumps deoxygenated blood to the lungs 3. Left Atrium - receives oxygenated blood from the lungs 4. Deoxygenated blood from the body enters the right atrium through the superior and inferior vena cava. 2. Right atrium contracts, pushing blood through the tricuspid valve into the right ventricle. 3. This is called double circulation because blood passes through the heart twice in one complete cycle - once through the right side (pulmonary circulation) and once through the left side (systemic circulation).

NEWTON'S THREE LAWS OF MOTION: FIRST LAW OF MOTION (Law of Inertia): Statement: An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion continues in motion with the same speed and in the same direction, unless acted upon by an unbalanced external force. This law defines inertia - the tendency of an object to resist changes in its state of motion. Daily Life Examples: 1. When a bus suddenly starts moving, passengers fall backward because their bodies tend to remain at rest due to inertia. 2. When you shake a wet umbrella, water droplets fly off because they tend to continue moving when the umbrella suddenly stops. SECOND LAW OF MOTION (Law of Acceleration): Statement: The rate of change of momentum of an object is directly proportional to the unbalanced force applied and takes place in the direction of the force. Mathematically: F = ma Where F = Force, m = mass, a = acceleration This law gives a quantitative measure of force. Daily Life Examples: 1. A cricket player moves his hands backward while catching a fast ball - this increases the time of impact, reducing the force and preventing injury. 2. It is easier to push an empty shopping cart than a full one - the same force produces less acceleration in a more massive object.
