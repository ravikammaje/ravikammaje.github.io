Section C: Long Answer Questions (5 × 4 = 20 Marks)

11. Rohan has 34 marbles. His friend gives him 28 more marbles. How many marbles does Rohan have in total? Show your working. [4]

Answer:

Step 1: Rohan's marbles = 34

Step 2: Marbles from friend = 28

Step 3: Total marbles = 34 + 28

Working: 34

+ 28

----

62

Rohan has 62 marbles in total.

Award: 1 mark for identifying the operation (addition), 1 mark for setting up correctly, 2 marks for correct answer (62).

12. A ribbon is 85 cm long. If 39 cm is cut off, how much ribbon is left? Draw a picture to show your answer. [4]

Answer:

Step 1: Total ribbon length = 85 cm

Step 2: Ribbon cut off = 39 cm

Step 3: Ribbon left = 85 - 39

Working: 85

- 39

----

46

[Drawing: Long ribbon (85 cm) → Cut portion (39 cm) | Remaining (46 cm)]

46 cm of ribbon is left.

Award: 1 mark for diagram, 1 mark for correct subtraction setup, 2 marks for correct answer (46 cm).

13. Complete the pattern and explain the rule: 15, 20, 25, 30, ___, ___, ___ [4]

Answer:

Step 1: Find the pattern

15 → 20 (add 5)

20 → 25 (add 5)

25 → 30 (add 5)

Step 2: Continue the pattern:

30 + 5 = 35

35 + 5 = 40

40 + 5 = 45

The next three numbers are: 35, 40, 45

Rule: Add 5 to get the next number (skip counting by 5)

Award: 1 mark for identifying the rule (add 5), 1 mark for each correct number (3 marks).

14. In a class, there are 26 boys and 29 girls. How many students are there in the class altogether? If 18 students are absent, how many students are present? [4]

Answer:

Part A: Total students

Boys = 26, Girls = 29

Total = 26 + 29 = 55 students

Part B: Students present

Total students = 55

Absent students = 18

Present students = 55 - 18 = 37 students

Total students = 55, Students present = 37

Award: 2 marks for correct total (55), 2 marks for correct number present (37).

15. A table is 2 metres long. Write its length in centimetres. If a pencil is 15 cm long, how many pencils placed end to end would equal the length of the table? [4]

Answer:

Part A: Convert metres to centimetres

1 metre = 100 cm

2 metres = 2 × 100 = 200 cm

Part B: Number of pencils

Table length = 200 cm

Pencil length = 15 cm

Number of pencils = 200 ÷ 15

200 ÷ 15 = 13 remainder 5

Table length = 200 cm

13 pencils can fit completely (with 5 cm left over)

Award: 2 marks for correct conversion (200 cm), 2 marks for correct division (13 pencils). Accept 13 or "13 with remainder" as correct.
