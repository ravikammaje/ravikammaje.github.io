Section A: Multiple Choice Questions (5 x 2 = 10 Marks)

1. Which is a solid? Science

Answer: c) Ice

Ice is the solid form of water. Water and steam are liquid and gas forms.

2. Number of continents Geography

Answer: c) 7

There are 7 continents: Asia, Africa, North America, South America, Antarctica, Europe, Australia.

3. Bird that dances in rain Science

Answer: b) Peacock

The peacock is famous for its colorful feathers and dance during the rainy season.

4. Earth rotates once in Geography

Answer: b) 24 hours

Earth completes one rotation on its axis in 24 hours, causing day and night.

5. Insect that makes honey Science

Answer: c) Honeybee

Honeybees collect nectar from flowers and make honey in their hives.

Section B: Short Answer Questions (5 x 2 = 10 Marks)

1. Three states of matter with examples Science

Solid: Wood, Stone, Ice, Book

Liquid: Water, Milk, Juice, Oil

Gas: Air, Oxygen, Carbon dioxide, Steam

2. Largest and smallest continents Geography

Largest continent: Asia

Smallest continent: Australia (Oceania)

3. Migratory birds Science

Migratory birds are birds that travel long distances from one place to another in search of food and a suitable climate during certain seasons.

Examples: Siberian Crane, Greater Flamingo, Painted Stork, Arctic Tern

4. Why day and night occur Geography

The Earth rotates (spins) on its axis once every 24 hours.

The side of Earth facing the Sun has day.

The side of Earth away from the Sun has night.

As Earth rotates, different parts experience day and night.

5. Useful and harmful insects Science

Useful insects: Honeybee (gives honey), Silkworm (gives silk), Butterfly (helps in pollination), Ladybug (eats harmful insects)

Harmful insects: Mosquito (spreads diseases), Housefly (spreads germs), Locust (destroys crops), Cockroach (spreads diseases)

Section C: Long Answer Questions (5 x 4 = 20 Marks)

1. Forms of Matter: Science

a) When ice is heated: Ice changes into water (liquid). This change is called melting.

b) When water is heated strongly: Water changes into steam (water vapor/gas). This change is called evaporation or boiling.

c) Properties:

Solids: (i) Have a fixed shape (ii) Have a fixed volume (iii) Cannot be compressed

Liquids: (i) Take the shape of the container (ii) Have a fixed volume (iii) Can flow

d) Compressing air: Yes, air can be compressed into a smaller space.

Examples: Air in bicycle pump, air in footballs, compressed air in tires, scuba diving tanks

2. Continents and Oceans: Geography

a) Seven continents:

1. Asia 2. Africa 3. North America 4. South America 5. Antarctica 6. Europe 7. Australia (Oceania)

b) Five oceans:

1. Pacific Ocean 2. Atlantic Ocean 3. Indian Ocean 4. Southern Ocean (Antarctic) 5. Arctic Ocean

c) Largest ocean: Pacific Ocean

Smallest ocean: Arctic Ocean

d) India is located on the continent of Asia.

Countries sharing borders with India: Pakistan, China, Nepal, Bhutan, Bangladesh, Myanmar (any two)

3. Birds - Types and Features: Science

a) Flightless birds: Ostrich, Penguin, Emu, Kiwi

They use their strong legs for running fast (ostrich, emu) or swimming (penguin).

b) Different beaks: Birds have different beaks suited to the type of food they eat.

- Eagle: Sharp, hooked beak to tear meat

- Sparrow: Short, strong beak to crack seeds

- Hummingbird: Long, thin beak to sip nectar from flowers

- Pelican: Pouch-like beak to scoop fish

c) Duck vs Eagle feet:

- Duck: Webbed feet (toes connected by skin) for swimming in water

- Eagle: Sharp, curved claws (talons) for catching and holding prey

d) Nest: A nest is a home that birds build to lay eggs and raise their young.

- Weaver bird: Builds a hanging nest woven from grass

- Tailor bird: Stitches leaves together to make a nest

- Sparrow: Builds a cup-shaped nest with twigs and grass

4. Earth's Rotation and Seasons: Geography

a) Shape of Earth: The Earth is shaped like a sphere (slightly flattened at poles - called a geoid).

The model of Earth is called a Globe.

b) Two movements of Earth:

- Rotation: Earth spins on its axis (once in 24 hours). This causes day and night.

- Revolution: Earth moves around the Sun (once in 365 days or 1 year). This causes seasons.

c) Why we have seasons:

The Earth is tilted on its axis. As Earth revolves around the Sun, different parts of Earth receive different amounts of sunlight at different times of the year. This causes the seasons (summer, winter, spring, autumn).

d) Four main directions: North, South, East, West

If you face the rising Sun (East), your left hand points to the North.

5. Insects - Structure and Life Cycle: Science

a) Insect body:

Insects have 6 legs (3 pairs).

Three main body parts: Head, Thorax, Abdomen

b) Life cycle of butterfly (4 stages):

1. Egg - Butterfly lays eggs on leaves

2. Caterpillar (Larva) - Egg hatches into a caterpillar that eats leaves

3. Pupa (Chrysalis) - Caterpillar forms a hard case around itself

4. Adult Butterfly - Beautiful butterfly emerges from the pupa

This type of life cycle is called metamorphosis.

c) Housefly is harmful:

- Sits on garbage and dirty things, then sits on our food

- Spreads germs and diseases like typhoid, cholera, diarrhea

Protection: Cover food, use mosquito nets, keep surroundings clean, use dustbins

d) Insects and their importance:

(i) Gives us silk: Silkworm

(ii) Helps in pollination: Butterfly / Honeybee

(iii) Destroys crops: Locust / Grasshopper

(iv) Spreads malaria: Mosquito (Anopheles mosquito)
