Section A: Multiple Choice Questions (5 x 2 = 10 Marks)

1. NOT a renewable source of energy

Answer: c) Coal

Coal is a fossil fuel that takes millions of years to form and cannot be renewed quickly.

2. Process by which plants make food using sunlight

Answer: b) Photosynthesis

Photosynthesis uses sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide to produce glucose and oxygen.

3. The "Red Planet"

Answer: c) Mars

Mars appears red due to iron oxide (rust) on its surface.

4. Number of bones in human body

Answer: c) 206

An adult human has 206 bones in their skeletal system.

5. Solid changing directly to gas

Answer: c) Sublimation

Example: Dry ice (solid CO₂) changing directly to gas, camphor balls.

Section B: Short Answer Questions (5 x 2 = 10 Marks)

1. Three main parts of a cell and their functions

1. Cell membrane: Controls what enters and leaves the cell (acts as a protective boundary)

2. Nucleus: Controls all cell activities and contains genetic material (DNA)

3. Cytoplasm: Jelly-like substance where all chemical reactions take place and organelles are suspended

2. Food chain definition and example

Definition: A food chain is a linear sequence showing how energy and nutrients flow from one organism to another in an ecosystem.

Example: Grass → Grasshopper → Frog → Snake → Hawk

(Producer → Primary consumer → Secondary consumer → Tertiary consumer → Top predator)

3. Conductor vs Insulator

Conductor: A material that allows electricity to flow through it easily.

Example: Copper, Iron, Aluminum

Insulator: A material that does not allow electricity to flow through it easily.

Example: Rubber, Plastic, Wood, Glass

4. Three types of rocks with examples

1. Igneous rocks: Formed from cooled magma/lava. Example: Granite, Basalt

2. Sedimentary rocks: Formed from deposits of sediments over time. Example: Sandstone, Limestone

3. Metamorphic rocks: Formed from other rocks changed by heat and pressure. Example: Marble, Slate

5. Water cycle and its stages

Definition: The water cycle is the continuous movement of water on, above, and below the Earth's surface through different processes.

Four stages:

1. Evaporation: Water from oceans, lakes, rivers turns into water vapor

2. Condensation: Water vapor cools and forms clouds

3. Precipitation: Water falls as rain, snow, or hail

4. Collection: Water collects in rivers, lakes, oceans, or seeps into groundwater

Section C: Long Answer Questions (5 x 4 = 20 Marks)

1. The Human Body - Circulatory System:

a) Main function:

The circulatory system transports oxygen, nutrients, and hormones to cells throughout the body and removes waste products like carbon dioxide from cells.

b) Four chambers of the heart:

1. Right Atrium (upper right)

2. Right Ventricle (lower right)

3. Left Atrium (upper left)

4. Left Ventricle (lower left)

c) Difference between arteries and veins:

• Arteries carry oxygenated blood away from the heart (except pulmonary artery)

• Veins carry deoxygenated blood towards the heart (except pulmonary vein)

• Arteries have thick, elastic walls; veins have thin walls with valves

d) Heart as a "double pump":

The heart acts as a double pump because it has two separate pumping systems:

• Right side pumps deoxygenated blood to the lungs (pulmonary circulation)

• Left side pumps oxygenated blood to the rest of the body (systemic circulation)

2. Ecosystems and Environment:

a) Ecosystem definition and examples:

An ecosystem is a community of living organisms interacting with each other and their non-living environment.

Examples: Forest ecosystem, Pond ecosystem, Desert ecosystem, Coral reef ecosystem

b) Producer, Consumer, and Decomposer:

• Producer: Makes own food through photosynthesis. Example: Plants, algae

• Consumer: Cannot make own food, eats other organisms. Example: Rabbit (herbivore), Lion (carnivore)

• Decomposer: Breaks down dead organisms and waste. Example: Bacteria, Fungi

c) Biodegradable vs Non-biodegradable waste:

• Biodegradable: Can be broken down by microorganisms naturally. Examples: Food waste, Paper, Cotton cloth, Wood

• Non-biodegradable: Cannot be broken down naturally. Examples: Plastic, Glass, Styrofoam, Metal cans

d) Effects of deforestation:

1. Loss of wildlife habitat and biodiversity

2. Soil erosion and flooding

3. Climate change due to increased carbon dioxide

4. Disruption of water cycle

3. Light and Sound:

a) Luminous vs Non-luminous objects:

• Luminous objects: Emit their own light. Examples: Sun, Stars, Light bulb, Fire

• Non-luminous objects: Do not emit their own light. Examples: Moon, Book, Chair, Mirror

b) How we see non-luminous objects:

Light from a luminous source (like the Sun or a lamp) falls on the object. The object reflects this light into our eyes. Our eyes receive this reflected light, and the brain interprets it as an image.

c) Echo:

An echo is the repetition of sound caused by the reflection of sound waves from a hard surface.

Conditions for hearing an echo:

• The reflecting surface should be at least 17 meters away

• The surface should be hard and large enough

• The original sound should be loud enough

d) Sound vs Light in vacuum:

• Sound is a mechanical wave that needs a medium (solid, liquid, or gas) to travel. In vacuum, there are no particles to vibrate, so sound cannot travel.

• Light is an electromagnetic wave that does not need a medium and can travel through vacuum.

4. Simple Machines and Force:

a) Simple machine definition and types:

A simple machine is a device that makes work easier by changing the direction or magnitude of a force.

Types: 1. Lever 2. Pulley 3. Wheel and axle 4. Inclined plane 5. Wedge 6. Screw

b) Lever and its classes:

A lever is a rigid bar that rotates around a fixed point called the fulcrum.

• Class 1: Fulcrum between effort and load. Example: See-saw, Scissors

• Class 2: Load between fulcrum and effort. Example: Wheelbarrow, Nutcracker

• Class 3: Effort between fulcrum and load. Example: Tweezers, Fishing rod

c) How a pulley makes work easier:

A pulley changes the direction of the applied force, allowing us to pull down instead of lifting up. Multiple pulleys (pulley system) can also reduce the amount of force needed by distributing the load.

d) Work done calculation:

Work = Force × Distance

Work = 50 N × 4 m = 200 Joules (J)

5. Earth and Space:

a) Day and night:

Day and night occur because Earth rotates on its axis. One complete rotation takes approximately 24 hours. The side of Earth facing the Sun experiences day (receives sunlight), while the side facing away from the Sun experiences night (in shadow).

b) Seasons:

Seasons change because:

• Earth revolves around the Sun in about 365¼ days

• Earth's axis is tilted at 23.5 degrees

• This tilt causes different parts of Earth to receive varying amounts of sunlight during the year

• When a hemisphere tilts towards the Sun, it experiences summer; when tilted away, it experiences winter

c) Solar vs Lunar eclipse:

• Solar eclipse: Moon comes between Earth and Sun, blocking sunlight from reaching Earth. Occurs during new moon.

• Lunar eclipse: Earth comes between Sun and Moon, casting a shadow on the Moon. Occurs during full moon.

d) Natural satellites and their planets:

1. Moon - Earth

2. Phobos - Mars

3. Europa - Jupiter

4. Titan - Saturn

(Also accept: Ganymede-Jupiter, Callisto-Jupiter, Io-Jupiter, Deimos-Mars, Triton-Neptune)
