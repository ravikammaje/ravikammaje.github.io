Section C: Long Answer Questions (5 x 4 = 20 Marks)

Answer the following questions in detail in your notebook.

1. Force and Pressure: Physics [4]

a) Define force. What are contact forces and non-contact forces? Give one example of each.

b) A force of 200 N acts on an area of 0.5 m². Calculate the pressure exerted.

c) Why are the tips of nails and pins made sharp? Explain with reference to pressure.

d) The atmospheric pressure at sea level is about 101,325 Pa. Why don't we feel crushed by this pressure?

2. Physical and Chemical Changes: Chemistry [4]

a) List four characteristics of a chemical change.

b) Classify the following as physical or chemical changes: (i) Rusting of iron (ii) Melting of ice (iii) Digestion of food (iv) Dissolving sugar in water

c) When you heat a candle, what type of change occurs? Explain what happens during this process.

d) Why is cooking considered a chemical change? Give two reasons.

3. Electricity and Magnetism: Physics [4]

a) What is an electric current? Define conductors and insulators with two examples each.

b) Draw a simple circuit diagram showing a battery, bulb, switch, and connecting wires. Label all components.

c) Explain the difference between series and parallel circuits. In which type does the current remain the same throughout?

d) What is an electromagnet? How is it different from a permanent magnet? State one use of electromagnets.

4. Health and Hygiene: Biology [4]

a) What is a communicable disease? Name two diseases spread by: (i) contaminated water (ii) air

b) What is vaccination? How does it help in preventing diseases?

c) Write any four preventive measures to avoid mosquito-borne diseases.

d) What is the role of white blood cells in fighting infections? What is immunity?

5. Industries and Energy Resources: Geography [4]

a) What are agro-based industries? Name two agro-based industries in India and their raw materials.

b) Differentiate between conventional and non-conventional sources of energy with two examples each.

c) Why is solar energy considered the energy of the future? What are its advantages?

d) Name the major iron and steel producing centers in India. What factors influence the location of iron and steel industries?
