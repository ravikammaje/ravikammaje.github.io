Section B: Short Answer Questions - Solutions

1. Why do camels have broad, flat feet? Physics

Camels have broad, flat feet to reduce pressure on the sand.

Since Pressure = Force/Area:

- Larger area of feet → Lower pressure on sand

- This prevents their feet from sinking into the soft desert sand

- The same weight is distributed over a larger surface area

2. Differences between physical and chemical change Chemistry

Physical Change Chemical Change
No new substance is formed New substance is formed
Usually reversible Usually irreversible

Example of Physical change: Melting of ice

Example of Chemical change: Rusting of iron

3. Electric circuit and its types Physics

An electric circuit is a closed path through which electric current flows.

Two types:

1. Series Circuit: Components are connected end-to-end; same current flows through all components.

2. Parallel Circuit: Components are connected across each other; voltage is same across all components.

4. Causative organism and transmission Biology

(i) Malaria:

- Causative organism: Plasmodium (protozoan)

- Mode of transmission: Bite of infected female Anopheles mosquito

(ii) Tuberculosis (TB):

- Causative organism: Mycobacterium tuberculosis (bacteria)

- Mode of transmission: Airborne droplets (coughing, sneezing)

5. Renewable sources of energy Geography

Renewable sources of energy are sources that can be replenished naturally and are inexhaustible.

Examples:

1. Solar energy (from the Sun)

2. Wind energy (from wind)

Other examples: Hydropower, Geothermal, Tidal, Biomass

Section C: Long Answer Questions - Detailed Solutions

1. Force and Pressure Physics [4]

a) Force definitions:

Force: A push or pull that can change the state of motion, shape, or direction of an object.

Contact forces: Forces that require physical contact. Example: Friction, Muscular force

Non-contact forces: Forces that act from a distance without touching. Example: Gravitational force, Magnetic force

b) Pressure calculation:

Given: Force (F) = 200 N, Area (A) = 0.5 m²

Pressure = Force / Area

P = 200 / 0.5

P = 400 Pa (Pascals) or 400 N/m²

c) Sharp tips of nails and pins:

Nails and pins have sharp tips to increase pressure.

Since P = F/A, for a given force:

- Smaller area → Greater pressure

- Sharp tip has very small area, so pressure is very high

- This high pressure helps penetrate surfaces easily

d) Why we don't feel atmospheric pressure:

We don't feel crushed because:

1. The pressure inside our body equals atmospheric pressure

2. Atmospheric pressure acts equally in all directions

3. Our body has evolved to withstand this pressure

4. The pressure is balanced from inside and outside

2. Physical and Chemical Changes Chemistry [4]

a) Four characteristics of chemical change:

1. New substance(s) with different properties is formed

2. Usually irreversible (cannot be reversed easily)

3. Energy is either absorbed or released (heat, light, sound)

4. Change in chemical composition occurs

Other characteristics: Color change, gas evolution, precipitate formation

b) Classification of changes:

(i) Rusting of iron → Chemical change (new substance Fe₂O₃ formed)

(ii) Melting of ice → Physical change (only state changes, H₂O remains)

(iii) Digestion of food → Chemical change (food broken into nutrients)

(iv) Dissolving sugar in water → Physical change (can be recovered by evaporation)

c) Heating a candle:

Both physical and chemical changes occur:

Physical change: Wax melts near the flame (solid → liquid)

Chemical change: Wax vapor burns to produce carbon dioxide, water vapor, heat and light

The burning (combustion) is a chemical change as new substances are formed

d) Why cooking is a chemical change:

Reasons:

1. New substances with different taste, color, and smell are formed

2. The change is irreversible (cooked food cannot become raw again)

3. Chemical composition of ingredients changes

4. Heat energy causes chemical reactions in food

3. Electricity and Magnetism Physics [4]

a) Definitions:

Electric current: The flow of electric charge (electrons) through a conductor. Measured in Amperes (A).

Conductors: Materials that allow electric current to pass through easily.

Examples: Copper, Aluminum, Iron, Silver

Insulators: Materials that do not allow electric current to pass through.

Examples: Rubber, Plastic, Glass, Wood

b) Circuit diagram:

[Students should draw a circuit with:]

- Battery (cell) with + and - terminals

- Bulb in the circuit

- Switch (open or closed position)

- Connecting wires joining all components in a closed loop

- All components properly labeled

c) Series vs Parallel circuits:

Series Circuit Parallel Circuit
Components connected end-to-end Components connected across each other
Same current through all Same voltage across all
If one fails, all stop working Others continue if one fails

In series circuit, current remains the same throughout.

d) Electromagnet:

An electromagnet is a temporary magnet made by passing electric current through a coil wound around a soft iron core.

Difference from permanent magnet:

- Electromagnet can be switched on/off; permanent magnet cannot

- Electromagnet's strength can be varied; permanent magnet's strength is fixed

Use: Electric bells, cranes for lifting heavy iron/steel, speakers

4. Health and Hygiene Biology [4]

a) Communicable disease:

A communicable disease is a disease that can spread from one person to another through direct or indirect contact.

Diseases spread by contaminated water: Cholera, Typhoid, Dysentery, Hepatitis A

Diseases spread by air: Tuberculosis, Common cold, Influenza, COVID-19

b) Vaccination:

Vaccination is the process of introducing weakened or killed pathogens (or their parts) into the body to stimulate the immune system.

How it helps:

1. Body produces antibodies against the pathogen

2. Memory cells are formed that remember the pathogen

3. If the actual pathogen enters later, body responds quickly

4. Provides long-term immunity without causing the disease

c) Prevention of mosquito-borne diseases:

1. Do not allow water to stagnate; drain/cover water containers

2. Use mosquito nets while sleeping

3. Apply mosquito repellent creams/sprays

4. Wear full-sleeved clothes, especially during dawn and dusk

Other measures: Use mosquito screens on windows, spray insecticides, keep surroundings clean

d) White blood cells and immunity:

Role of WBCs:

1. WBCs (leukocytes) are the body's defense cells

2. They engulf and destroy pathogens (phagocytosis)

3. They produce antibodies to neutralize pathogens

4. They create memory cells for future protection

Immunity: The ability of the body to resist infection and fight against disease-causing organisms (pathogens).

5. Industries and Energy Resources Geography [4]

a) Agro-based industries:

Agro-based industries are industries that use agricultural products as raw materials.

Examples:

1. Sugar industry - Raw material: Sugarcane

2. Cotton textile industry - Raw material: Cotton

3. Jute industry - Raw material: Jute

b) Conventional vs Non-conventional sources:

Conventional Sources Non-conventional Sources
Used for a long time traditionally Recently developed, modern sources
Mostly non-renewable, exhaustible Renewable, inexhaustible
Examples: Coal, Petroleum Examples: Solar, Wind energy

c) Solar energy - the future:

Solar energy is considered the energy of the future because:

1. It is renewable and inexhaustible

2. It is clean and pollution-free

3. Available freely in abundant quantities

4. Can be harnessed in remote areas

5. Low maintenance cost after installation

6. Reduces dependence on fossil fuels

d) Iron and steel centers:

Major centers: Jamshedpur (Jharkhand), Bhilai (Chhattisgarh), Durgapur (West Bengal), Rourkela (Odisha), Bokaro (Jharkhand), Visakhapatnam (Andhra Pradesh)

Factors influencing location:

1. Availability of raw materials (iron ore, coal, limestone)

2. Cheap transportation (railways, waterways)

3. Availability of water for cooling

4. Availability of labor

5. Market proximity
