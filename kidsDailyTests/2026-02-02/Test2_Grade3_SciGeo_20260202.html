Section C: Long Answer Questions (5 x 4 = 20 Marks)

Answer the following questions in detail in your notebook.

1. Forms of Matter: Science [4]

a) What happens to ice when it is heated? What is this change called?

b) What happens to water when it is heated strongly? What is this change called?

c) Give two properties of solids and two properties of liquids.

d) Can you compress (squeeze) air into a smaller space? Give an example from daily life.

2. Continents and Oceans: Geography [4]

a) Name all seven continents of the world.

b) Name all five oceans of the world.

c) Which ocean is the largest? Which ocean is the smallest?

d) On which continent is India located? Name two countries that share borders with India.

3. Birds - Types and Features: Science [4]

a) Name two birds that cannot fly. What do they use their legs for?

b) Why do birds have different types of beaks? Give two examples showing how beak shape helps birds eat.

c) How are a duck's feet different from an eagle's feet? How does this help each bird?

d) What is a nest? Name two birds and describe the type of nests they build.

4. Earth's Rotation and Seasons: Geography [4]

a) What is the shape of the Earth? What do we call the model of the Earth?

b) The Earth has two types of movements. Name them and explain what each movement causes.

c) Why do we have different seasons (summer, winter, etc.) on Earth?

d) Name the four main directions. If you face the rising Sun, which direction is on your left?

5. Insects - Structure and Life Cycle: Science [4]

a) How many legs does an insect have? Name the three main body parts of an insect.

b) Describe the life cycle of a butterfly. Draw and label the four stages.

c) How is a housefly harmful to humans? How can we protect ourselves from flies?

d) Name the insect that: (i) gives us silk (ii) helps in pollination (iii) destroys crops (iv) spreads malaria
