Plants and Animals

Section A: Multiple Choice Questions (5 × 2 = 10 Marks)

1. Which part of the plant makes food?

Answer: c) Leaves

Leaves make food using sunlight, water, and air (photosynthesis).

2. What do plants need to grow?

Answer: c) Water, sunlight, air and soil

Plants need all four: water, sunlight, air, and soil to grow healthy.

3. Which animal gives us milk?

Answer: b) Cow

Cows give us milk. Other milk-giving animals are buffalo, goat.

4. A butterfly is an _______.

Answer: c) Insect

A butterfly has 6 legs and 3 body parts, making it an insect.

5. Which part of the plant holds it firmly in the soil?

Answer: c) Roots

Roots anchor the plant in soil and absorb water and nutrients.

Section C: Long Answer Questions (5 × 4 = 20 Marks)

11. Draw and label the four main parts of a plant. Write one function of each part. [4]

Answer:

Four main parts and their functions:

1. Roots: Absorb water and minerals from soil, hold the plant firmly in the ground

2. Stem: Carries water and minerals from roots to leaves, supports the plant

3. Leaves: Make food for the plant using sunlight (photosynthesis)

4. Flower: Helps the plant reproduce, becomes fruit with seeds

Award: 1 mark for correct diagram with labels, 1 mark for any 3 correct functions.

12. How do animals help us? Write any four ways with examples. [4]

Answer:

Animals help us in many ways:

1. Food: Cow gives milk, hen gives eggs, fish gives meat

2. Transport: Horses, donkeys, and camels carry people and goods

3. Protection: Dogs guard our homes

4. Clothing: Sheep gives wool, silkworm gives silk

5. Farming: Ox helps plough fields, bees help in pollination

6. Companionship: Pets like dogs and cats are our friends

Award 1 mark for each correct way with example (any 4).

13. Why are plants important for us? Write any four reasons. [4]

Answer:

Plants are important because:

1. Oxygen: Plants give us fresh air (oxygen) to breathe

2. Food: Plants give us fruits, vegetables, grains, and pulses to eat

3. Shelter: Trees provide wood to build houses and furniture

4. Medicines: Many plants are used to make medicines

5. Shade: Trees give us shade on hot days

6. Paper: Wood from trees is used to make paper

7. Rain: Trees help bring rain

Award 1 mark for each correct reason with brief explanation (any 4).

14. Sort these animals into two groups - animals that live on land and animals that live in water: Fish, Dog, Whale, Cat, Frog, Octopus, Tiger, Shark. Which animal can live in both places? [4]

Answer:

Land Animals: Dog, Cat, Tiger

Water Animals: Fish, Whale, Octopus, Shark

Lives in Both (Amphibian): Frog

Explanation: A frog can live in water (as a tadpole and can swim) and on land (as an adult frog). Frogs are called amphibians.

Award: 1 mark for correct land animals, 1 mark for correct water animals, 2 marks for identifying frog as the animal that lives in both places.

15. Riya planted a seed in a pot. What does the seed need to grow into a healthy plant? Describe the journey of a seed becoming a plant. [4]

Answer:

What the seed needs:

1. Water

2. Sunlight

3. Air

4. Good soil with nutrients

Journey of a seed becoming a plant:

Step 1: The seed is planted in soil

Step 2: When watered, the seed absorbs water and swells

Step 3: A tiny root comes out and grows down into the soil

Step 4: A small shoot grows up towards the sunlight

Step 5: The shoot breaks through the soil surface

Step 6: Small leaves appear on the shoot

Step 7: The plant grows bigger with more leaves

Step 8: Eventually, flowers and fruits may grow

Award: 2 marks for listing what seed needs, 2 marks for describing the growth stages.
