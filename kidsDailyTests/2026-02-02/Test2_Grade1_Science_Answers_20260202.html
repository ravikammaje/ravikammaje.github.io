ICSE Grade 1 - Science (EVS) Test

Plants and Animals

ANSWER KEY

Date: 02 February 2026
Time: 25-30 minutes
Total Marks: 40
Section A: Multiple Choice Questions (5 × 2 = 10 Marks)

1. Which part of the plant grows under the ground?

Answer: c) Root

Roots grow underground and absorb water.

2. Which animal gives us milk?

Answer: b) Cow

Cows, buffaloes, and goats give us milk.

3. What do plants need to grow?

Answer: b) Water and sunlight

Plants need water, sunlight, air, and soil.

4. Which animal can fly?

Answer: c) Sparrow

Sparrow is a bird that can fly. Dogs, cats, and fish cannot fly.

5. What is the green part of a plant called?

Answer: b) Leaf

Leaves are green and make food for the plant.

10. What comes from a seed when we plant it? [2]

Answer:

A plant (or seedling/sapling) grows from a seed when we plant it in soil and give it water.

Accept: plant, baby plant, seedling, or sapling.

Section C: Long Answer Questions (5 × 4 = 20 Marks)

11. Draw a plant and label these parts: root, stem, leaf, flower. [4]

Answer:

A correctly drawn plant should show:

  • Root - at the bottom, underground (absorbs water)
  • Stem - middle part, holds the plant up (carries water)
  • Leaf - growing from stem, green in color (makes food)
  • Flower - at the top, colorful (makes seeds)

Award 1 mark for each correctly labeled part.

12. Write the names of 4 pet animals. Why do people keep pets at home? [4]

Answer:

4 Pet Animals:

  1. Dog
  2. Cat
  3. Fish
  4. Parrot / Rabbit / Hamster

Why people keep pets:

  • Pets are our friends and give us company
  • They make us happy
  • Dogs guard our homes
  • We can play with them

Award 2 marks for naming 4 pets, 2 marks for reason.

13. How do we take care of plants? Write any 4 ways. [4]

Answer:

4 ways to take care of plants:

  1. Water the plants regularly
  2. Keep plants in sunlight
  3. Remove weeds from the soil
  4. Do not pluck leaves or flowers unnecessarily
  5. Add manure or fertilizer to help them grow
  6. Protect plants from insects

Award 1 mark for each correct way (any 4).
