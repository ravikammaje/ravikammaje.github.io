3. Importance of Sun for plants Science

The Sun is important for plants because:

- Plants need sunlight to make their food

- Plants make food through a process called photosynthesis

- Using sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide, plants make glucose (sugar) which is their food

- They also release oxygen which we need to breathe

4. Germs and diseases Science

Germs: Germs are tiny living things (microorganisms) that are so small we cannot see them with our eyes. They can cause diseases when they enter our body.

Diseases spread through dirty hands:

1. Diarrhea (loose motions)

2. Typhoid

3. Cholera

4. Food poisoning (any two)

5. Pulses Science

Dal, rajma, and chana belong to the Pulses food group.

Pulses are seeds of plants that give us proteins which help our body grow and repair.

More examples: Moong dal, Urad dal, Masoor dal, Chickpeas (chole), Kidney beans (rajma), Black-eyed peas (lobia)

1. Living and Non-living Things: Science

a) Main characteristics of living things (any four):

1. Need food: All living things need food for energy

2. Breathe: Living things breathe in oxygen and release carbon dioxide

3. Grow: Living things grow and develop over time

4. Move: Living things can move on their own

5. Reproduce: Living things can have babies/offspring

6. Respond: Living things respond to changes around them

7. Excrete: Living things remove waste from their body

b) Examples of non-living things:

Natural non-living things: Water, Air, Stones, Mountains, Sunlight

Man-made non-living things: Chair, Table, Car, Books, Computer

No, a car is not a living thing even though it can move because:

- A car cannot move on its own; it needs fuel and a driver

- A car does not breathe, eat, grow, or have babies

- A car is man-made, not born

Living things: Tree, Frog, Bird

3. The Sun - Our Star: Science

a) What is the Sun and its importance:

The Sun is a star - a huge ball of hot, burning gases. It is the closest star to Earth.

Importance for life on Earth:

- Gives us light so we can see during the day

- Gives us heat to keep the Earth warm

- Helps plants make food (photosynthesis)

- Controls the water cycle

- Without the Sun, there would be no life on Earth!

b) Sun and the water cycle:

1. The Sun heats water in rivers, lakes, and oceans

2. The water evaporates (turns into water vapor) and rises up

3. The water vapor cools and forms clouds (condensation)

4. Clouds release water as rain (precipitation)

This cycle continues because of the Sun's heat.

c) Solar energy:

Solar energy is the energy we get from the Sun's light and heat.

Uses of solar energy:

1. Solar cookers - to cook food

2. Solar panels - to make electricity

3. Solar water heaters - to heat water

4. Drying clothes - wet clothes dry in sunlight

5. Drying food items (like papad, pickles)

d) Safety from the Sun:

Why not look directly at the Sun: The Sun's light is very bright and can damage our eyes permanently. It can cause blindness.

Precautions on sunny days:

- Wear a hat or cap

- Use sunglasses

- Apply sunscreen on skin

- Carry an umbrella

- Drink plenty of water

- Avoid going out in peak afternoon heat

4. Cleanliness, Health and Hygiene: Science

a) Personal hygiene:

Personal hygiene means keeping our body clean and healthy to prevent diseases.

Four good habits of personal hygiene:

1. Bathing daily with soap

2. Brushing teeth twice a day (morning and night)

3. Washing hands before eating and after using the toilet

4. Wearing clean clothes

5. Keeping hair clean and combed

6. Cutting nails regularly

b) When to wash hands (four important times):

1. Before eating food

2. After using the toilet

3. After playing outside

4. After touching animals or pets

5. After coughing or sneezing

6. Before and after touching a sick person

c) Keeping surroundings clean:

1. Throw garbage only in dustbins

2. Do not spit on roads or public places

3. Do not let water collect in containers (prevents mosquitoes)

4. Keep drains clean and covered

5. Plant trees and maintain gardens

d) Importance of healthy habits:

(i) Cover mouth when coughing: To stop germs from spreading to others through air droplets

(ii) Drink clean water: Dirty water contains germs that cause diseases like cholera, typhoid, and diarrhea

(iii) Cut nails regularly: Dirt and germs collect under long nails and can enter our body when we eat with dirty hands
