1. (-18) - (-25) + (-7)

= (-18) + 25 + (-7)

= -18 + 25 - 7

= 7 - 7

= 0

2. (34 × 32) ÷ 35

Using law: am × an = am+n

= 34+2 ÷ 35

= 36 ÷ 35

Using law: am ÷ an = am-n

= 36-5 = 31 = 3

3. Time for Rs 4,000 to become Rs 5,200 at 6% SI

Amount = Rs 5,200, Principal = Rs 4,000

SI = Amount - Principal = 5,200 - 4,000 = Rs 1,200

SI = (P × R × T)/100

1,200 = (4,000 × 6 × T)/100

1,200 = 240 × T

T = 1,200/240 = 5 years

4. If angle 1 = 65°, find angles 2 and 3

Angle 2 (corresponding angle): = 65° (corresponding angles are equal)

Angle 3 (co-interior angle): = 180° - 65° = 115°

(Co-interior angles are supplementary, i.e., they add up to 180°)

5. Perimeter of square with area 144 cm2

Area of square = side2 = 144 cm2

Side = √144 = 12 cm

Perimeter = 4 × side = 4 × 12 = 48 cm

4. Parallel Lines and Transversal:

a) Definitions:

(i) Corresponding angles: Angles that are on the same side of the transversal and in corresponding positions (one above the parallel line, one below). They are equal when lines are parallel.

(ii) Alternate interior angles: Angles that are on opposite sides of the transversal and between the parallel lines. They are equal when lines are parallel.

b) Co-interior angles:

Co-interior angles (same-side interior angles) are supplementary.

If one angle = 72°

Other angle = 180° - 72° = 108°

c) Finding angle QTS:

PQ || RS, angle PQT = 110°, angle RST = 130°

In triangle QTS:

Angle TQS = 180° - 110° = 70° (linear pair)

Angle TSR = 180° - 130° = 50° (linear pair)

Angle QTS = 180° - 70° - 50° = 60°

d) All angles when transversal makes 55° with line l:

At line l: 55°, 125°, 55°, 125° (vertically opposite and linear pairs)

At line m (same pattern): 55°, 125°, 55°, 125°

(Corresponding angles are equal)

5. Perimeter and Area:

a) Rectangle (15 cm × 9 cm):

Area = l × b = 15 × 9 = 135 cm2

Perimeter = 2(l + b) = 2(15 + 9) = 2 × 24 = 48 cm

b) Square with perimeter 64 cm:

Perimeter = 4 × side = 64

Side = 64/4 = 16 cm

Area = side2 = 162 = 256 cm2

c) Area of path:

Outer rectangle: 25 m × 18 m

Path width = 2 m inside

Inner rectangle: (25 - 2 - 2) × (18 - 2 - 2) = 21 m × 14 m

Area of garden = 25 × 18 = 450 m2

Area of inner part = 21 × 14 = 294 m2

Area of path = 450 - 294 = 156 m2

d) Area of triangle:

Area = (1/2) × base × height

Area = (1/2) × 16 × 11 = 88 cm2
