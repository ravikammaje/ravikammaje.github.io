Section C: Long Answer Questions (5 x 4 = 20 Marks)

Show all your working clearly in your notebook.

1. Place Value and Number Names:

a) Write the greatest 4-digit number using the digits 3, 7, 1, 9 (use each digit only once).

b) Write the smallest 4-digit number using the same digits.

c) Arrange these numbers from smallest to largest: 5,632 ; 5,362 ; 5,236 ; 5,623

d) What is the difference between the place value and face value of 5 in 3,527? [4]

2. Multiplication Word Problems:

a) A packet has 6 chocolates. Rohan buys 11 such packets. How many chocolates does he have in all?

b) There are 12 rows in a hall. Each row has 8 chairs. How many chairs are there in the hall?

c) Find the product: 47 x 6. Show the step-by-step working.

d) A book costs Rs 35. What is the cost of 9 such books? [4]

3. 2D Shapes and Symmetry:

a) Name a 2D shape that has: (i) 3 sides (ii) 5 sides (iii) 6 sides

b) Draw a circle. How many lines of symmetry does a circle have?

c) Look at these letters: A, B, H, M, T. Which letters have a vertical line of symmetry?

d) Draw an equilateral triangle. How many lines of symmetry does it have? Draw them. [4]

4. Measurement - Length and Weight:

a) Convert: (i) 4 km 250 m to meters (ii) 5,680 m to km and m

b) Anu walked 1 km 350 m in the morning and 2 km 700 m in the evening. How much did she walk in total?

c) Convert: (i) 3 kg 750 g to grams (ii) 6,200 g to kg and g

d) A watermelon weighs 4 kg 200 g. A mango weighs 350 g. How much heavier is the watermelon than 5 mangoes? [4]

5. Number Patterns:

a) Write the next three numbers: 100, 95, 90, 85, ___, ___, ___

b) Complete the pattern: 2, 4, 8, 16, ___, ___. What is the rule?

c) Fill in the missing numbers: 7, 14, ___, 28, 35, ___

d) Create your own pattern starting with 5, using the rule "add 6 each time". Write the first 6 numbers. [4]
