Section C: Long Answer Questions (5 x 4 = 20 Marks)

Show all your working clearly in your notebook.

1. Sets:

Let U = {x : x is a natural number less than 15}

A = {x : x is a multiple of 3 less than 15}

B = {x : x is an even number less than 15}

a) Write sets U, A, and B in roster form.

b) Find A ∪ B (union of A and B).

c) Find A ∩ B (intersection of A and B).

d) Find A' ∩ B' (intersection of complements). [4]

2. Fractions and Decimals:

a) Arrange in ascending order: 2/3, 5/8, 7/12, 3/4

b) A pipe fills 2/5 of a tank in one hour. If the tank is currently 1/4 full, what fraction of the tank will be filled after 2 hours?

c) Convert 2.45̄ (2.4555...) to a fraction.

d) Simplify: (3.5 × 0.8) ÷ 0.14 [4]

3. Linear Equations:

a) Solve: (2x + 3)/5 = (x - 2)/3

b) The sum of two consecutive odd numbers is 36. Find the numbers.

c) A number is 7 more than another number. If their sum is 43, find both numbers.

d) Ravi's age is 5 years more than twice Priya's age. If the sum of their ages is 32 years, find their ages. [4]

4. Congruence and Symmetry:

a) In triangles ABC and PQR, AB = PQ = 5 cm, BC = QR = 7 cm, and ∠B = ∠Q = 60°. Are the triangles congruent? If yes, state the congruence criterion used.

b) How many lines of symmetry does an isosceles triangle have? What about an equilateral triangle?

c) A rectangle has dimensions 8 cm × 6 cm. What is its order of rotational symmetry? At what angles does it map onto itself?

d) ΔABC ≅ ΔDEF by SSS criterion. If AB = 4 cm, BC = 6 cm, and AC = 5 cm, write down the lengths of DE, EF, and DF. [4]

5. Probability:

A bag contains 4 red balls, 6 green balls, and 5 yellow balls.

a) A ball is drawn at random. Find the probability that it is: (i) green (ii) not yellow

b) If 2 red balls are added to the bag, what is the new probability of drawing a red ball?

c) Two dice are thrown simultaneously. Find the probability of getting a sum of 7.

d) A coin is tossed three times. Find the probability of getting: (i) exactly two heads (ii) at least one head [4]
