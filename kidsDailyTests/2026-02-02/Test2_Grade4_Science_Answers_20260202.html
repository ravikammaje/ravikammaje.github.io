Grade 4 ICSE Science Test - ANSWER KEY

Date: 02 February 2026

Total Marks: 40
Time: 25-30 minutes
Section A: Multiple Choice Questions (5 x 2 = 10 Marks)

1. Teeth used for tearing food

Answer: b) Canines

Canines are sharp and pointed, used for tearing food.

2. Organ that produces bile juice

Answer: c) Liver

The liver produces bile juice which helps digest fats.

3. Friction can be reduced by

Answer: b) Oil

Oil acts as a lubricant and reduces friction between surfaces.

4. Cactus stores water in

Answer: c) Stem

Cactus has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water.

5. Gas we breathe in

Answer: c) Oxygen

We breathe in oxygen and breathe out carbon dioxide.

Section B: Short Answer Questions (5 x 2 = 10 Marks)

1. Balanced diet and energy-giving nutrients

Balanced diet: A diet that contains all the nutrients (carbohydrates, proteins, fats, vitamins, minerals, water, and roughage) in the right amounts needed by our body.

Energy-giving nutrients: Carbohydrates and Fats

2. Why brush teeth twice a day

We need to brush our teeth twice a day to:

- Remove food particles stuck between teeth

- Prevent bacteria from forming plaque

- Prevent cavities and tooth decay

- Keep breath fresh and gums healthy

3. Organs of excretory system

1. Kidneys: Filter blood and remove waste as urine

2. Skin: Removes waste through sweat

(Other correct answers: Lungs - remove carbon dioxide; Urinary bladder - stores urine)

4. Transparent and opaque objects

Transparent objects (light passes through completely):

- Glass, clear water, air

Opaque objects (light cannot pass through):

- Wood, metal, brick, stone

5. Push and Pull

Push: When we move an object away from us by applying force.

Example: Pushing a door to close it, pushing a shopping cart

Pull: When we move an object towards us by applying force.

Example: Pulling a drawer to open it, pulling a rope

Section C: Long Answer Questions (5 x 4 = 20 Marks)

1. The Teeth and Dental Care:

a) Four types of teeth:

1. Incisors - for cutting and biting

2. Canines - for tearing food

3. Premolars - for crushing food

4. Molars - for grinding food

b) Number of teeth:

- Adult: 32 teeth (permanent teeth)

- Child: 20 teeth (milk teeth or baby teeth)

c) Causes of cavities:

- Eating too many sweets and sugary foods

- Not brushing teeth properly

- Bacteria in the mouth produce acid that damages tooth enamel

d) Good habits for healthy teeth:

1. Brush teeth twice daily (morning and night)

2. Avoid eating too many sweets and chocolates

3. Rinse mouth after every meal

4. Visit dentist regularly for check-ups

5. Eat calcium-rich foods like milk and cheese

2. The Digestive System:

a) Organs in correct order:

Mouth → Food pipe (Oesophagus) → Stomach → Liver → Small intestine → Large intestine → Rectum → Anus

b) What happens in the stomach:

- Food is mixed with digestive juices (gastric juice)

- The stomach churns and breaks down food into a semi-liquid form

- Proteins begin to be digested here

c) Importance of chewing food properly:

- Breaks food into smaller pieces

- Mixes food with saliva which starts digestion

- Makes it easier for stomach to digest

- Prevents choking and stomach problems

d) Role of small intestine:

- Completes the digestion of food

- Absorbs digested nutrients into the blood

- Has tiny finger-like projections called villi that increase surface area for absorption

3. Adaptations in Plants:

a) Adaptation in plants:

Adaptation means special features or characteristics that help plants survive in their environment (habitat).

b) Desert plant adaptations (cactus):

1. Thick, fleshy stem to store water

2. Leaves are modified into spines to reduce water loss

3. Waxy coating on stem to prevent water evaporation

4. Long roots to absorb water from deep underground

c) Aquatic plants and adaptations:

Examples: Lotus, Water hyacinth, Water lily

Adaptations:

- Have floating leaves with waxy coating to stay above water

- Stems have air spaces to help them float

- Roots are reduced or absent

d) Plants in hilly areas:

- Cone-shaped trees (like pine) so snow slides off easily

- Needle-like leaves to reduce water loss

- Flexible branches to bend with strong winds

- Deep roots to hold firmly in the soil

4. Air and Its Properties:

a) Three gases in air:

1. Nitrogen (about 78%)

2. Oxygen (about 21%)

3. Carbon dioxide (small amount)

(Also: Water vapour, other gases)

b) Activity to prove air occupies space:

Take an empty glass and push it upside down into a bucket of water. The water does not enter the glass completely because air inside the glass occupies space and prevents water from entering. This proves air occupies space.

c) Importance of oxygen:

- Needed for breathing by all living things

- Required for burning (combustion)

- Helps release energy from food in our body

- Without oxygen, living things cannot survive

d) Air pollution - causes and prevention:

Causes:

1. Smoke from vehicles and factories

2. Burning of garbage and fossil fuels

Ways to reduce:

1. Plant more trees (trees purify air)

2. Use public transport or cycle

3. Avoid burning garbage

4. Use clean energy sources

5. Force and Friction:

a) Definition of force and its effects:

Force is a push or pull that can change the position, shape, or speed of an object.

Effects of force:

- Can make a stationary object move

- Can stop a moving object

- Can change the direction of a moving object

- Can change the shape of an object

b) Friction and useful examples:

Friction is a force that opposes motion when two surfaces rub against each other.

Useful examples:

1. Friction between shoes and ground helps us walk without slipping

2. Brakes use friction to stop vehicles

3. Writing with a pencil - friction between pencil and paper

c) Powder on carrom board:

We sprinkle powder (boric powder) on a carrom board to reduce friction between the striker/coins and the board surface. This helps the coins slide smoothly and travel further.

d) Other types of forces:

1. Gravitational force: Force that pulls objects towards Earth. Example: An apple falling from a tree

2. Muscular force: Force applied by muscles. Example: Lifting a bag, kicking a ball

3. Magnetic force: Force exerted by magnets. Example: A magnet attracting iron nails
