2. Fractions and Decimals [4]

a) Ascending order:

Convert to equivalent fractions with LCM of 3, 8, 12, 4 = 24

2/3 = 16/24, 5/8 = 15/24, 7/12 = 14/24, 3/4 = 18/24

Ascending order: 7/12, 5/8, 2/3, 3/4

b) Tank problem:

Tank filled per hour = 2/5

In 2 hours = 2 × (2/5) = 4/5

Current level = 1/4

After 2 hours = 1/4 + 4/5 = 5/20 + 16/20 = 21/20

= 21/20 or 1 1/20 (tank will be full and overflow by 1/20)

c) Convert 2.45̄ to fraction:

Let x = 2.4555...

10x = 24.555...

100x = 245.555...

100x - 10x = 245.555... - 24.555...

90x = 221

x = 221/90

d) Simplify (3.5 × 0.8) ÷ 0.14:

3.5 × 0.8 = 2.8

2.8 ÷ 0.14 = 280/14

= 20

3. Linear Equations [4]

a) Solve (2x + 3)/5 = (x - 2)/3:

Cross multiply: 3(2x + 3) = 5(x - 2)

6x + 9 = 5x - 10

6x - 5x = -10 - 9

x = -19

b) Consecutive odd numbers:

Let the numbers be (2n - 1) and (2n + 1)

(2n - 1) + (2n + 1) = 36

4n = 36, n = 9

The numbers are 17 and 19

c) Sum of two numbers:

Let smaller number = x, larger = x + 7

x + (x + 7) = 43

2x = 36, x = 18

The numbers are 18 and 25

d) Ages problem:

Let Priya's age = x, Ravi's age = 2x + 5

x + (2x + 5) = 32

3x = 27, x = 9

Priya's age = 9 years, Ravi's age = 23 years

4. Congruence and Symmetry [4]

a) Congruence of triangles:

AB = PQ = 5 cm (Side)

∠B = ∠Q = 60° (Included Angle)

BC = QR = 7 cm (Side)

Yes, triangles are congruent by SAS (Side-Angle-Side) criterion.

b) Lines of symmetry:

Isosceles triangle: 1 line of symmetry (along the altitude from vertex to base)

Equilateral triangle: 3 lines of symmetry (along each altitude)

c) Rectangle symmetry:

Order of rotational symmetry = 2

It maps onto itself at 180° and 360°

d) Congruent triangles:

Since ΔABC ≅ ΔDEF by SSS:

AB corresponds to DE, BC corresponds to EF, AC corresponds to DF

DE = 4 cm, EF = 6 cm, DF = 5 cm

5. Probability [4]

a) Probability from bag:

Total balls = 4 + 6 + 5 = 15

(i) P(green) = 6/15 = 2/5

(ii) P(not yellow) = (4 + 6)/15 = 10/15 = 2/3

b) After adding 2 red balls:

New total = 15 + 2 = 17

New red balls = 4 + 2 = 6

P(red) = 6/17

c) Sum of 7 with two dice:

Total outcomes = 6 × 6 = 36

Favorable outcomes for sum 7: (1,6), (2,5), (3,4), (4,3), (5,2), (6,1) = 6

P(sum = 7) = 6/36 = 1/6

d) Three coin tosses:

Total outcomes = 2³ = 8 (HHH, HHT, HTH, THH, HTT, THT, TTH, TTT)

(i) P(exactly 2 heads) = 3/8 (HHT, HTH, THH)

P(at least one head) = 1 - P(no heads) = 1 - P(TTT) = 1 - 1/8

(ii) P(at least one head) = 7/8
