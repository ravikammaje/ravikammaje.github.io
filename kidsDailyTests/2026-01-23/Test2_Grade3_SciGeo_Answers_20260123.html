Section C: Long Answer Questions (20 Marks)

11. The Water Cycle:
The water cycle is the continuous movement of water on, above, and below Earth's surface.
• Evaporation: The sun heats water in rivers, lakes, and oceans, turning it into water vapor (gas) that rises into the air.
• Condensation: As water vapor rises and cools in the atmosphere, it turns back into tiny water droplets, forming clouds.
• Precipitation: When clouds become heavy with water droplets, the water falls back to Earth as rain, snow, or hail.
The cycle then repeats as this water flows into water bodies or seeps into the ground.
Award full marks for mentioning all three processes with explanations.
12. Life Cycle of a Butterfly:
A butterfly goes through four stages, called complete metamorphosis:
1. Egg: The butterfly lays tiny eggs on leaves.
2. Larva (Caterpillar): The egg hatches into a caterpillar that eats leaves and grows.
3. Pupa (Chrysalis): The caterpillar forms a hard covering around itself and undergoes transformation inside.
4. Adult Butterfly: The adult butterfly emerges from the chrysalis with wings and can fly and reproduce.
Award marks for mentioning all four stages with brief descriptions.
13. Difference between Weather and Climate:
• Weather refers to the atmospheric conditions at a particular place and time. It can change from day to day or even hour to hour.
Example: Today it is sunny and warm, but tomorrow it might rain.
• Climate is the average weather pattern of a place over a long period of time (usually 30 years or more).
Example: Mumbai has a tropical climate with hot, humid summers and heavy monsoon rains.
Accept answers showing understanding of short-term vs. long-term patterns.
